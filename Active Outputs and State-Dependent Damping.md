# Active Outputs and State-Dependent Damping
## A First-Principles Argument

> A self-contained argument for the empirical claim on which a metabolic theory of health and disease stands or falls — and the deepest insight that follows from it. Reasoned from physics through molecular biology to clinical implication, with confidence calibrated per layer of evidence. Self-contained: no prior knowledge of the parent framework required.

---

## The Question That Matters

Most discourse about health asks the wrong question first. *What should I eat? How do I treat condition X? What supplement helps Y?* Each presupposes answers to a question almost no one asks: **what IS health, and what IS disease, as biological phenomena, at the deepest resolution biology currently allows?**

The answer matters. Without it, every clinical decision is local — addressing the symptom at hand without knowing whether you are treating the trunk of the tree or one of its leaves. With it, the leaves organize themselves.

This essay defends a single empirical claim and traces its consequences to a single clinical principle.

**The claim:** the load-bearing outputs of cellular metabolism — ATP, CO₂, and lactate — are not passive byproducts. They engage specific named molecular targets whose downstream consequences feed back to alter subsequent metabolism in the direction that sustains the producing state.

**The principle:** the regulatory machinery the body uses to oppose pathological state-shifts depends on the same outputs it should regulate, so the brakes degrade with the fuel that runs them.

If both hold, what we call "health" and "disease" are two self-reinforcing states the metabolic system can occupy under appropriate parameter conditions, and the practical question becomes how to shift the system from one to the other. If they don't hold, the framework offered here is wrong and should be discarded.

---

## What We Have to Accept Before We Begin

Three textbook claims that no informed party disputes:

**One.** A living organism is an ordered system. The second law of thermodynamics guarantees that order, against the gradient of entropy, requires continuous energy input. When energy production falls below the maintenance threshold, the system does not merely slow — it degrades, and the degradation produces conditions that further impair energy production. (Schrödinger 1944; Lehninger 2017.)

**Two.** ATP is the universal cellular energy currency. Every function a cell performs — ion gradient maintenance, protein synthesis, DNA repair, immune surveillance, hormone synthesis, neurotransmitter packaging, muscle contraction, vesicular trafficking, autophagy — requires ATP at one or more steps. A human body produces and consumes roughly 40 kg of ATP per day, approaching its own body weight. ATP turnover is not a side process; it is the central activity of being alive.

**Three.** Glucose enters glycolysis and is converted to pyruvate, which then forks. One path enters the mitochondria, where pyruvate is fully oxidized through the Krebs cycle and electron transport chain to produce CO₂, water, and ~36 ATP. The other path stays in the cytoplasm, where pyruvate is reduced to lactate to produce 2 ATP. The asymmetry is **18-fold**. This is the difference between a cell that runs its full machinery and a cell that must triage — abandoning repair, immune surveillance, and differentiation to sustain bare survival.

These three premises are bedrock. We can build on them.

---

## A Short Deduction That Reorganizes Everything

The machinery that produces ATP — the electron transport chain (~100+ subunits), F₀F₁ ATP synthase, the Krebs-cycle enzymes, the cardiolipin-rich inner mitochondrial membrane, the ribosomes that synthesize all of these, the chaperones that fold them, the autophagy machinery that removes the damaged copies — is itself maintained by ATP-dependent processes. **The producer maintains itself.**

This is not a hypothesis. It is bookkeeping, downstream of premise 2. But notice what it implies: **self-reinforcement is structural to life, not coincidental.** Energy production creates the conditions for its own continuation or degradation. A system whose order requires energy and whose energy production requires its order to be maintained is, by structural necessity, *circular*. It will tend to amplify its own current state.

This circularity is why the question of health and disease can have an architectural answer rather than a list of independent diseases. If self-reinforcement is structural, the question is not *whether* the metabolic system has self-reinforcing states but *what* maintains them — what specific outputs of metabolism shape the conditions for their own continuation.

---

## The Binary Question Everything Hinges On

Given the circular dependency, the products of metabolism (ATP, CO₂, lactate) face a single binary question:

- Are they merely consumed, cleared, and disposed — passive byproducts of a machine that runs and maintains itself locally — without specific downstream feedback to the producing system?
- Or do they engage specific molecular targets whose responses feed back to alter the subsequent state of metabolism?

The principle of parsimony does not decide this. Some products of metabolism *are* genuine passive byproducts — heat, water, urea in mammals, ammonia in fish. The question must be settled empirically, mechanism by mechanism, with primary literature.

This is the load-bearing question of any first-principles framework for chronic disease. **If outputs are passive,** chronic disease shows a metabolic *signature* but the signature is not the *engine*. Treatment can address the underlying pathology and metabolic markers will follow. **If outputs are active,** the markers are also the agents. Chronic disease feeds itself through them, and any treatment that does not interrupt the active feedback works against the system's self-reinforcing dynamics.

The remainder of the argument addresses this question with specific evidence.

---

## What This Argument Does Not Claim

Before presenting the evidence, six clarifications about scope. Each preempts a misreading the framework's structure does not actually contain.

**Not that ALL metabolic outputs are active.** Heat, water, ammonia (in fish), urea (in mammals), and most catabolic intermediates are or can be passive byproducts depending on context. The claim is restricted to the load-bearing outputs at the pyruvate fork: ATP, CO₂, and lactate.

**Not that active outputs are the only regulators of metabolism.** Hormonal regulators (T3, insulin, glucagon, cortisol, estrogen, progesterone), neural regulators (sympathetic/parasympathetic), substrate regulators (glucose, fatty acids, amino acids), and genetic regulators all exist and matter. The claim is that they operate *through* metabolic capacity for chronic disease, not that they don't exist.

**Not that every chronic disease reduces to this dynamic alone.** The universal-architecture layer is bounded to primary-driver and central-pathway modes. The peripheral category (cystic fibrosis, fibrillin-1/Marfan, syndromic genetic neurodevelopmental conditions like Rett and Fragile X) is explicitly out-of-scope. *Non-metabolic initiating lesion does not mean non-metabolic disease* — but some diseases have neither initiating nor cascading metabolic involvement, and per-disease verification remains the appropriate empirical work.

**Not that mitochondrial restoration is a specific intervention modality.** M restoration is the *state that must be produced* for downstream regulation to recover. The lever can be input-side (reducing substrate overload), output-side (directly restoring oxidative capacity), substrate-side (reducing damage substrates like tissue PUFA), rate-side (T3 optimization), or load-side (chronic stress reduction). The principle is that this outcome must occur, not that any specific intervention class comes first.

**Not that lactate is bad, PUFA is bad, estrogen is bad, or any categorical claim about specific molecules.** Control architecture and timescale determine direction. Acute exercise-induced lactylation is reparative; chronic uncontrolled-drift lactylation is pathogenic — same molecular biology, opposite outcomes. Acute PUFA replacement of saturated fat can improve blood lipid profiles short-term; chronic PUFA tissue accumulation damages mitochondrial cardiolipin over decades — different timescales, different endpoints. Estrogen protects metabolically intact cells and damages metabolically compromised cells — same molecule, opposite effects determined by attractor state.

**Not that functional metabolic markers replace standard diagnostics.** They complement standard hormone testing by capturing tissue-level metabolic state that serum hormone levels can miss. They are additive diagnostic information, not a replacement diagnostic framework.

These clarifications are constraints, not hedges. Each narrows what the argument can be invoked to claim, making it easier to falsify rather than harder.

## The Mechanistic Evidence

Seven specific molecular mechanisms, each independently characterized in the peer-reviewed literature, none built toward this framework.

### 1. ATP and V-ATPase: Mediator Sequestration

V-ATPase is a rotary proton pump composed of ~14 subunits across two domains, V₁ (cytosolic, ATP-binding) and V₀ (membrane-embedded, proton-translocating). It sits in the limiting membrane of secretory granules in mast cells and platelets and uses ATP hydrolysis to maintain a ~1.9-unit pH gradient against cytosol — granule interior ~5.3–5.5 against cytosol ~7.2. The acidic interior is required to keep monoamine mediators (histamine, serotonin) in their protonated form, ionically complexed with the heparin/serglycin proteoglycan storage matrix.

When cytosolic ATP falls — through chronic mitochondrial suppression or pharmacological inhibition by bafilomycin A1 — V-ATPase pumping slows, granule interior pH rises, the protonated-ionic mediator/matrix complex destabilizes, and stored histamine escapes into the extracellular space. Paradkar et al. (2017, *Cell Death & Disease*, PMID 28492555) demonstrated this directly: bafilomycin treatment of mast cells produces granule alkalinization, vacuole-like morphology, intracellular histamine reduction, and concurrent extracellular histamine increase.

The downstream consequence: released histamine activates H₁/H₂/H₃/H₄ receptors with effects on vasodilation, immune polarization, and tissue inflammation. Released serotonin activates 5-HT receptors with effects including vasoconstriction, mitochondrial inhibition, and metabolic suppression. The net effect on tissue oxidative metabolism is suppressive. Lower tissue ATP further compromises V-ATPase, perpetuating storage failure.

ATP is not a passively-consumed currency in this context. It is the agent that maintains an active subcellular structure whose state determines whether mediators that suppress subsequent metabolism are sequestered or released.

### 2. CO₂ and the Bohr Effect: Targeted Oxygen Delivery

CO₂ produced by Krebs-cycle dehydrogenases enters erythrocytes, is converted to bicarbonate by carbonic anhydrase (releasing H⁺), and the H⁺ binds histidine residues on hemoglobin's α and β subunits — stabilizing the T-state (low oxygen affinity) relative to R-state (high oxygen affinity). Direct CO₂ binding to N-terminal amino groups (carbamino formation) provides a second contribution to the same shift. The conjunction shifts the hemoglobin-O₂ binding curve rightward in tissues with high oxidative metabolism, releasing oxygen preferentially where it is being consumed (Bohr, Hasselbalch, Krogh 1904; Perutz 1970; Jensen 2004).

The feedback to subsequent metabolism is direct. Oxygen supply is rate-limiting for OXPHOS at most physiological loads. The Bohr effect ensures supply tracks demand spatially: tissues sustaining oxidative metabolism receive proportionally more O₂; tissues falling out of oxidative metabolism receive proportionally less. The output of successful oxidation directs more substrate to where oxidation is succeeding.

This is the cleanest case where CO₂ itself is the active agent at a specific molecular target — characterized at residue resolution and with hemoglobin's allosteric structure solved at atomic resolution. CO₂ has additional active mechanisms (bicarbonate activation of soluble adenylyl cyclase, carbamylation of connexin 26 K125, carbamylation of ubiquitin K48), but the Bohr effect alone is sufficient.

### 3. Lactate and Histone Lactylation: Transcriptional Reprogramming

In 2019, Zhang et al. (*Nature*, PMID 31645732) discovered that lactate covalently modifies histone lysines through a chemically distinct modification (lysine lactylation, Kla) — separate from acetylation, methylation, or phosphorylation. They identified 28 lactylation sites on core histones.

The downstream consequence is the transcriptional reprogramming of the cell toward the producing metabolic state. Histone lactylation activates expression of glycolytic enzymes (LDHA, HK2, PKM2), driving more lactate production from the glucose now flowing through the upregulated pathway. In tumors and chronically inflamed tissue, lactylation drives immune-evasion and fibrosis programs. Pan et al. (2022, *Cell Metabolism*, PMID 35303422) directly characterized the positive-feedback loop in vivo in Alzheimer's microglia: chronic glycolytic shift → elevated lactate → H4K12 lactylation at glycolytic-gene promoters → transcriptional activation of glycolysis → continued lactate production. Inhibition of the loop ameliorated amyloid-β burden and cognitive deficits in 5XFAD mice.

This is the cleanest molecular case of an output reshaping the conditions for its own continuation. The byproduct of glycolysis directly drives expression of the enzymes of glycolysis. The modification was undiscovered until 2019; the framework's directional claim that lactate is an active disease driver predates the molecular discovery, but the discovery confirmed the claim at a level of specificity the framework had not predicted.

The control-architecture distinction matters here: acute exercise-induced lactylation (Sun 2025, *Nat Commun*) is reparative — programmatic entry, intact termination, return to baseline. Chronic uncontrolled-drift lactylation is pathogenic. The same molecular biology produces opposite outcomes determined by the control architecture, not the molecule.

### 4. Lactate, HIF-1α, and Macrophage Polarization

Lactate stabilizes HIF-1α independent of oxygen tension by inhibiting prolyl hydroxylases and through direct effects on HIF-1α stability. HIF-1α drives transcription of glycolytic enzymes (HK2, LDHA, PKM2), GLUT1 glucose transporter, MCT4 lactate exporter, and angiogenic factors. In tissue macrophages, lactate-stabilized HIF-1α drives polarization toward immunosuppressive M2 phenotypes (Colegio et al. 2014, *Nature*, PMID 25043024).

The cross-cellular feedback loop: chronic tissue lactate → HIF-1α stabilization in resident macrophages → M2 polarization → immunosuppression + fibrosis + impaired perfusion → tissue hypoxia + sustained glycolytic flux → continued lactate production. This complements lactylation: lactate acts both intracellularly via histone modification and across cellular boundaries via HIF-1α stabilization in neighboring cells. Two independent active mechanisms at different molecular targets, both sustaining the producing state.

### 5. ATP and Tight Junction Integrity: The Gut Barrier

Intestinal epithelial tight junctions seal the paracellular space between adjacent enterocytes. Their integrity requires continuous ATP-dependent processes — actomyosin contraction through MLCK-mediated MLC phosphorylation, ATP-dependent vesicular trafficking of TJ proteins (occludin, claudins), and Na+/K+-ATPase-mediated maintenance of cellular ion gradients (Madara 1998; Turner 2009).

JanssenDuijghuijsen et al. (2017, *Front Physiol*, PMID 29075202) directly tested mitochondrial ATP as the load-bearing variable: human Caco-2 monolayers in oxidative phenotype, treated with mitochondrial OXPHOS inhibitors (CCCP, rotenone, piericidin A). Result: dose-dependent monolayer permeability increase, six-fold cellular ATP decrease, claudin 7 specifically internalizing from membrane to cytoplasm. Mitochondrial ATP production is load-bearing for intestinal barrier integrity, with claudin 7 trafficking as the specific load-bearing step.

The systemic feedback: low enterocyte ATP → TJ failure → endotoxin (LPS) translocation into portal circulation → TLR4 activation in liver → NF-κB-mediated inflammatory cytokines → systemic suppression of mitochondrial respiration → maintained low enterocyte ATP. Chronic endotoxinemia is a documented driver of insulin resistance, NAFLD, and metabolic syndrome.

### 6. Mitochondrial Output and Regulatory Machinery

This is the deepest mechanism and the bridge to state-dependent damping. Every regulatory mechanism in the body — HPT axis (TRH/TSH/T4/T3, deiodinases), HPA axis (CRH/ACTH/cortisol, 11β-HSD), antioxidant defenses (GSH, SOD, GPx, glutaredoxin/thioredoxin), mitochondrial biogenesis (PGC-1α, NRF1, NRF2, TFAM), autophagy and mitophagy (PINK1/Parkin, BNIP3, BCL2L13), immune resolution (specialized pro-resolving mediators — resolvins, protectins, maresins), hepatic regeneration, and lactate clearance — requires the active outputs to maintain itself.

- Protein synthesis (ribosomal translation, aminoacyl-tRNA synthesis, chaperone-assisted folding) is ATP-dependent at multiple steps.
- Protein trafficking via motor proteins is ATP-dependent.
- Cofactor regeneration: GSSG → GSH requires NADPH; oxidized thioredoxin → reduced thioredoxin requires NADPH; sirtuins require NAD+.
- Substrate import and export across mitochondrial membranes require Δψ_m.
- Quality control through autophagy is ATP-dependent at multiple steps.

The regulatory *act* may be thermodynamically free (allosteric inhibition, product inhibition, simple binding). The regulatory *machinery* is always ATP/NADPH/Δψ_m-dependent for synthesis, folding, trafficking, and cofactor regeneration. Keep the machinery intact, regulation runs. Let it degrade, and regulation fails regardless of whether the act itself would have consumed ATP.

### 7. Clinical Validation: Two Independent Drugs Reverse the Same Disease From Opposite Ends of the Same Loop

In March 2024, the FDA approved resmetirom (Rezdiffra) for noncirrhotic MASH/NASH with fibrosis. Mechanism: liver-selective THR-β agonism → upregulated mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation → restored hepatic oxidative capacity. Direct restoration of the failed output. MAESTRO-NASH (Harrison et al. 2024, *NEJM*, PMID 38324483): NASH resolution 26-30% vs. 10% placebo; fibrosis improvement 24-26% vs. 14%.

In August 2025, the FDA approved Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg) for the same disease. Mechanism: GLP-1 receptor agonism → substantial weight reduction (mean -10.5% vs -2.0% placebo) → reduced hepatic substrate (fatty acid) overload + improved glycemic control + improved cardiometabolic profile. Reduction of the input that overwhelmed the system. ESSENCE phase 3 (Newsome, Sanyal et al. 2025, *NEJM*, PMID 40305708): MASH resolution 63% vs. 34% placebo; fibrosis improvement 37% vs. 22%.

Two FDA-approved drugs, developed independently by different pharmaceutical companies with no theoretical alignment to this framework, reverse the same chronic liver disease through mechanisms operating at *opposite ends* of the predicted loop architecture. Output-side restoration and input-side reduction — both crossing the threshold from disease to health attractor through biologically multi-loop interventions delivered as single clinical interventions. Drug development optimized for revenue, not theoretical commitment, converged on the framework's predicted pattern from independent directions.

## The Convergence Argument

These mechanisms were discovered by researchers in different specialties at different times: mast cell biology (Pejler group, Paradkar/Akula), respiratory physiology (Bohr, Hasselbalch, Krogh, Perutz), epigenetics (Zhao group at U Chicago), cancer immunology (Medzhitov group at Yale), mucosal immunology and epithelial biology (Madara, Turner, JanssenDuijghuijsen), mitochondrial bioenergetics (Wallace, Mootha), and pharmaceutical R&D (Madrigal, Novo Nordisk).

Different funding sources. Different reviewers. Different journals. Different methodological traditions. Different communities of citation. **None of these researchers built toward an integrated metabolic theory of health and disease.** The Bohr effect predates any modern metabolic framework by a century. Histone lactylation was discovered decades after first-principles thinkers had already made the directional claim that lactate is an active disease driver. Pharmaceutical companies optimized for revenue, not theoretical alignment.

The convergence is the empirical security of active outputs. If active outputs were a theoretical commitment of the framework not grounded in the data, the mechanisms confirming it should not exist independently in the literature. They do exist independently, characterized at molecular resolution, and they converge on a single conclusion: **the load-bearing outputs of cellular metabolism are active.** They engage specific named molecular targets. They feed back to alter subsequent metabolism in the direction that sustains the producing state.

This is what active outputs claims. The evidence for it is not theoretical — it is the convergent product of decades of independent molecular biology that no specialty assembled into a single picture. The integration is the contribution; the evidence belongs to the researchers who produced it.

## State-Dependent Damping Follows

Once active outputs is established, state-dependent damping is not an additional empirical commitment — it is an inference that follows from active outputs plus textbook biology.

The argument is short:

- **Outputs are active.** ATP, CO₂, lactate engage specific targets with downstream feedback (established above).
- **Regulatory machinery requires the same active outputs to maintain itself.** Synthesis, folding, trafficking, and cofactor regeneration of every regulatory enzyme depend on ATP, NADPH, and Δψ_m (Mechanism 6 above).
- **Therefore: as outputs fall, regulatory machinery degrades.** The brakes that should oppose pathological state-shifts depend on the same fuel as the production they should regulate.

**Brakes degrade with the fuel that runs them.**

The cross-system unification is the framework's distinctive contribution. HPT axis, HPA axis, antioxidant defenses, mitochondrial biogenesis, autophagy, immune resolution, hepatic regeneration, lactate clearance — these are otherwise-independent regulatory systems studied by different specialties. They share one upstream substrate: the OXPHOS-coupled currencies. When mitochondrial respiratory capacity falls — for any reason: PUFA-mediated cardiolipin damage, iron-catalyzed peroxidation, chronic glycolytic shift, hypothyroidism, mitochondrial DNA depletion, proteostatic stress — the coupled currencies fall together, and damping degrades across systems in correlated rather than independent fashion.

The consequence is decisive. **Positive feedback does not defeat negative feedback through brute force. It operates by weakening it.** As the system drifts toward the disease state, mitochondrial function falls, the coupled currencies fall with it, and the mechanisms that would restore health degrade across the board. The net effective gain rises not because the positive feedback grew stronger but because the opposition collapsed. As damping currencies approach zero, the damping term approaches zero, producing the nonlinear gain structure characteristic of bistable transitions.

The clinical pattern follows directly. When a patient presents with simultaneous HPT dysfunction, impaired antioxidant defense, autophagy failure, immune dysregulation, and poor lactate clearance, the clustering is not coincidence and it is not five independent diagnoses requiring five independent interventions. They correlate because they share the OXPHOS substrate. **The unit of analysis is wrong** when these are treated as separate problems.

### What Is and Isn't Directly Tested

The components of state-dependent damping are textbook: ATP-dependent protein synthesis, NADPH-dependent antioxidant cofactor regeneration, Δψ_m-dependent mitochondrial protein import, NAD+-dependent sirtuin activity. The *synthesis* — that these dependencies share the OXPHOS origin and therefore degrade in correlated fashion when mitochondrial function fails, producing cross-system damping collapse — is the framework's specific claim.

This synthesis is supported by a broad pattern of clinical correlations across chronic disease but a direct test at the multi-system level (measuring all damping currencies and all regulatory functions in the same population with clear baselines) has not been performed. The framework treats it as high-confidence inference from textbook components, supported by clinical pattern, but acknowledges it as the largest open empirical commitment.

The NAD+ leg specifically requires tissue-specific qualification. Chubanava et al. (2025, *Cell Metabolism*) found 85% NAD+ depletion in adult mouse skeletal muscle without functional impairment, no acceleration of aging, and unchanged mitochondrial respiration. Dollerup et al. (2020, *Am J Clin Nutr*) found nicotinamide riboside supplementation failed to raise muscle NAD+ or improve mitochondrial function in human trial. The NAD+ component is well-supported preclinically in some tissues (liver, certain aging contexts) but contested in skeletal muscle and not reliably translated to humans via current supplementation pharmacology. The NADPH component is the cleanest case — pentose-phosphate-pathway-produced, ATP-independent, mechanistically specific for antioxidant regeneration. The ATP and Δψ_m components are uncontested. **The synthesis holds with the NAD+ leg held with appropriate uncertainty.**

## What This Means for Health and Disease

If active outputs is true and state-dependent damping is its consequence, the conventional picture of chronic disease is wrong about what generates it.

**Chronic disease is not a collection of independent pathologies that happen to share a metabolic signature.** It is the durable settling of a metabolic system into a self-reinforcing failure state, where outputs that should sustain function instead sustain dysfunction, and the regulatory mechanisms that should oppose this depend on the same failing outputs. The signature is the engine.

**The two states are attractors when parameter conditions create a disease basin.** When PUFA tissue burden is low, iron is well-regulated, chronic stress is absent, and gut barrier is intact, the organism is monostable — the healthy state is the only attractor and perturbations return to it. When parameter conditions deteriorate over time (years of dietary PUFA accumulation, iron loading, chronic stress, endotoxin exposure), the organism enters a bistable region where both attractors exist. Crossing into the disease attractor often appears sudden because it is — the system absorbs damage within the healthy attractor until the combined weight crosses the threshold where the disease attractor's reinforcing loops become self-sustaining.

**Disease progresses because the producer of the disease state is the disease state itself.** Lactate epigenetically reprograms cells toward more lactate production. ATP failure releases mediators that further suppress ATP production. Endotoxin damages the barrier that should exclude it. Each output reinforces the conditions that produced it.

**Single interventions plateau because the loops compensate.** Address one reinforcing loop in a multi-loop system, and the remaining loops continue producing the conditions that maintain the disease state. The temporary improvement followed by plateau or regression is the predictable signature of partial loop interruption in a multi-loop system.

**Mechanistically narrow interventions plateau even when delivered as combination therapy.** What matters is biological scope, not pill count. Bariatric surgery is one procedure but biologically multi-loop (caloric restriction + gut hormone reprogramming + bile acid alteration + microbiome shift + PUFA-displacement). Resmetirom is one drug but biologically multi-loop (TR-β agonism affects mitochondrial respiratory enzyme expression, fatty acid oxidation, lipogenesis suppression, LDL receptor upregulation simultaneously). Wegovy is one drug but biologically multi-loop (insulin sensitization, appetite suppression, hepatic substrate reduction, slow gastric emptying).

**Recovery requires breaking enough loops simultaneously that the remaining loops cannot compensate.** The threshold is crossed, not nudged. This is the inverse of why single interventions fail.

**Hysteresis is structural.** The threshold for entering disease and the threshold for exiting it differ. Bariatric remission rates depend on disease duration — equivalent surgical procedures and equivalent metabolic improvements produce different outcomes based on how long the slow variables have been in the disease basin (Schauer 2017 STAMPEDE 5-year). Critical slowing down — the longitudinal signature of approach to a saddle-node bifurcation — has been observed in the 3 years before diabetes diagnosis (Tabak 2009 Whitehall II, 10-15× acceleration in fasting glucose rise rate, specific to progressors). The system has memory.

**Some damage is structurally irreversible regardless of subsequent metabolic state.** Post-infarction cardiomyocyte loss, adult nephron loss, crosslinked fibrotic ECM, thermodynamically stable amyloid/tau/α-synuclein aggregates — all produced by prolonged metabolic failure but persisting independent of current metabolic state once formed. Metabolic restoration slows further damage; it does not reverse damage already done. Early intervention has disproportionate leverage not only because fewer feedback loops have engaged but because **structural ceilings on reversibility have not yet been crossed.**

## The Clinical Translation

State-dependent damping yields the framework's most actionable clinical principle:

**Mitochondrial respiratory capacity must be restored as a prerequisite, not as one option among many.**

You cannot fix HPT dysfunction, HPA dysregulation, antioxidant insufficiency, autophagy failure, immune dysregulation, or impaired lactate clearance as independent problems because each of them requires the synthesis/folding/trafficking/cofactor machinery that depends on the same OXPHOS-coupled currencies that are themselves failing. Try to fix them in parallel without restoring M, and you are running regulation through machinery that is itself degrading.

**The lever varies; the produced state is invariant.** M restoration is the *state that must occur* for downstream regulation to recover — not a specific intervention modality. Multiple levers produce it: input-side reduction (semaglutide reducing hepatic substrate overload), output-side restoration (resmetirom directly restoring oxidative capacity in a failing organ), substrate removal (PUFA reduction allowing existing cardiolipin to peroxidize less), rate restoration (T3 optimization upregulating respiratory enzyme expression), or load reduction (chronic stress management). All of these produce M restoration as outcome. The principle is that this outcome must be produced for everything else to recover, not that any specific intervention class must come first. The clinical question is which lever the patient's specific architecture responds to most.

This reorders intervention priority. The leverage points that actually move M:

**Reduce hardware damage substrate.** Polyunsaturated fatty acids accumulate in mitochondrial cardiolipin and peroxidize via iron-catalyzed Fenton chemistry, damaging Complex IV of the electron transport chain. Peroxidation chains are terminated by vitamin E. Reducing PUFA tissue burden, managing iron load, and ensuring adequate vitamin E status reduces ongoing hardware damage. The timescale is months to years — PUFA tissue half-life is on the order of years in adipose and ~600 days in cell membranes.

Note the variable and timescale specificity. The framework's claim operates at the level of chronic tissue accumulation (years-to-decades), mitochondrial cardiolipin composition, and peroxidation rate — not at the level of short-term dietary-fat-replacement studies measuring CVD events or blood lipid markers. Saturated-fat-to-PUFA replacement studies optimized for cardiovascular endpoints can show benefit on those endpoints (LDL reduction, CHD event reduction) while being neutral or harmful for tissue PUFA burden and mitochondrial integrity over decades. Endpoint divergence is the rule, not the exception. The recommendation here operates at the level the mechanism operates at: tissue burden, mitochondrial cardiolipin composition, peroxidation rate. It does not directly address the dietary-fat-replacement debate, which has its own evidence base on its own endpoints and operates on shorter timescales than the tissue-accumulation claim.

**Restore the metabolic rate-setter.** T3 controls respiratory enzyme expression at both the nuclear and mitochondrial genome levels. Resmetirom's clinical reversal of MASH through liver-selective THR-β agonism is the cleanest demonstration that restoring thyroid signaling restores oxidative capacity in a failing organ. Standard TSH-based diagnosis can miss tissue-level hypothyroid states because tissue thyroid signaling is locally regulated by deiodinases that can shift independent of serum hormone concentrations. Functional metabolic markers (basal metabolic rate, ETCO₂, body temperature, pulse) complement standard hormone testing rather than replacing it — they capture tissue-level metabolic state that TSH-based diagnosis can miss. The 2021 indirect-calorimetry case is illustrative: measured resting energy expenditure 39% below predicted triggered thyroid evaluation that confirmed undiagnosed hypothyroidism. Functional and standard markers are additive diagnostic information, not competing diagnostic frameworks.

**Support glucose oxidation through the pyruvate fork.** Pyruvate dehydrogenase requires B1, magnesium, and NAD+ as cofactors; deficiency in any blocks the metabolic fork at exactly the point where it forks. The Randle cycle (1963, confirmed in *Nat Rev Endocrinol* 2025): elevated free fatty acids block glucose oxidation at PDH. Reducing FFA mobilization (managing chronic stress, supporting bedtime nutrition to suppress nocturnal lipolysis) and ensuring PDH cofactor sufficiency unblock the fork.

**Maintain gut barrier integrity.** Endotoxin from gut bacteria, when translocated, activates every disease-attractor mediator simultaneously. Hypothyroidism slows gut motility (2.2-2.7× SIBO risk); supporting motility through thyroid optimization rather than probiotic colonization addresses the upstream cause.

**Oppose estrogen with progesterone where indicated.** Estrogen reprograms cells toward glycolysis through PI3K/AKT signaling. Progesterone supports mitochondrial respiration and opposes estrogen at every mechanistic point. The HPA axis suppresses gonadal progesterone via GnRH/LH inhibition under chronic stress (this is the actual mechanism — not "pregnenolone steal," which is biosynthetically incorrect).

**Discipline the daily oscillation.** Every 24 hours the organism enters a partial conservation state at night (cortisol up, FFA mobilization, T3 down, temperature down) and must reverse it each morning through two independent pathways: blue light → SCN → melatonin suppression, and red/near-infrared light → cytochrome c oxidase → ATP. **Long-term trajectory is determined by the daily balance of attractor occupancy** — not by which state the organism is "in." Insufficient morning light, deep nightly dips (driven by hypoglycemia → cortisol → FFA cascade), and inadequate metabolic capacity to exit the conservation state each morning shift the daily balance toward the disease attractor over weeks and months.

**Reset slow variables for durability.** Slow variables (PUFA tissue content, mitochondrial machinery integrity) determine the equilibria that fast variables (T3, estrogen, lactate) snap to. Interventions that reset only fast variables produce relapse-prone improvement; interventions that reset slow variables change the attractor landscape itself. Durable recovery requires slow-variable shifts.

**Intervene early.** Structural ceilings cannot be crossed back. Fewer feedback loops engaged means lower threshold to recovery; fewer structural ceilings crossed means higher ceiling on what recovery can restore. Early intervention has compound leverage: dynamical and structural together.

## Honest Calibration

The argument operates at three layers with distinct evidentiary footing. **Calibrate per layer.**

**The existence layer — very high confidence.** Active outputs is empirically secure. Seven independent mechanisms across multiple specialties, none built toward this framework, with two FDA-approved drug approvals reversing the same disease through opposite mechanisms in the predicted loop architecture. Disconfirmation here would require demonstrating that the named mechanisms (V-ATPase / granule maintenance, Bohr effect, lactylation, HIF-1α / M2, tight junction / ATP, regulatory-machinery / coupled-currency dependence) do not actually produce the claimed downstream consequences in physiologically relevant settings — which would require retraction or fundamental reinterpretation of multiple landmark studies across multiple fields.

**The disease-dynamics layer — high confidence in diabetes; supported elsewhere with alternative lock-in forms accommodated.** Formal bistability is empirically established in T2D through bariatric remission hysteresis (Schauer 2017 STAMPEDE 5-year — duration-dependent reversibility is a structural signature of bistable dynamics) and longitudinal critical slowing down (Tabak 2009 Whitehall II — 10-15× glucose-rise acceleration in the final ~3 years before diagnosis is the quantitative signature of approach to a saddle-node bifurcation). For other chronic disease (NAFLD, CVD, sarcopenia, age-related neurodegeneration, CKD), formal bistability is inferred by analogy. Alternative lock-in forms (structural irreversibility from fibrosis or cell loss, reserve exhaustion, nucleation-propagation in protein aggregates) are themselves produced by prolonged active-output failure but established by their own evidence. **The framework's practical architecture (multi-input intervention, disease-duration-dependent recovery, early-intervention leverage) survives regardless of which specific lock-in form is operative in any given disease.**

**The universal-architecture layer — bounded; requires per-disease verification.** The framework's chronic-disease scope:

- *Primary-driver mode* — metabolism is upstream cause and central pathway. Covers the bulk of chronic disease: T2D, MASH/NASH, CVD, sarcopenia, age-related neurodegeneration, CKD, increasingly functional psychiatric illness.
- *Central-pathway mode* — the initiating lesion is non-metabolic, but metabolism functions as a major axis in the pathogenic cascade alongside other mechanisms. Autoimmune disease (HLA/TCR susceptibility + metabolic clinical trigger), Huntington's (multi-mechanism cascade — transcriptional dysregulation, axonal transport defects, proteostasis failure, excitotoxicity, AND mHTT → TIM23 → OXPHOS dysfunction), hemoglobinopathies (ischemic energy-failure damage mechanism), channelopathies (metabolic state determines severity), chronic neuropathic pain (metabolic chronification of structural injury). **Non-metabolic initiating lesion does not mean non-metabolic disease — but metabolism is a major and clinically tractable axis in central-pathway diseases, not the sole pathogenic mechanism.**
- *Genuinely peripheral* — narrow category where metabolism is neither upstream nor central pathway: cystic fibrosis, fibrillin-1/Marfan, clearly syndromic genetic neurodevelopmental conditions (Rett, Fragile X). Explicitly out-of-scope, not a falsification target.

For most chronic disease, the pathogenic cascade routes through the active-output system whether or not the initiating event was metabolic. Per-disease verification — does this specific chronic disease's pathogenic cascade route through the active-output system? — remains the appropriate empirical work for extending Layer C to new domains.

**State-dependent damping at the cross-system unification level is the framework's largest open empirical commitment.** The components are textbook; the synthesis is inferred. A study measuring HPT, HPA, antioxidant defense, autophagy, immune resolution, regenerative capacity, and lactate clearance in the same population with clear baselines would confirm or refute the cross-system correlation directly. This study has not been performed.

A reader convinced of the existence layer but skeptical of the universal-architecture layer can engage productively per layer rather than rejecting wholesale. Conflating the layers manufactures apparent contradictions where none exist. **Each layer's confidence is calibrated to its evidence.**

## What We Now Hold

The strongest first-principles + evidence argument for a metabolic theory of health and disease can be stated as a single sentence:

> Because the machinery that produces cellular energy is itself maintained by energy, and because the load-bearing outputs of metabolism (ATP, CO₂, lactate) engage specific named molecular targets whose downstream consequences feed back to alter subsequent metabolism in the direction that sustains the producing state — and because every regulatory mechanism the body uses to oppose pathological state-shifts depends on the same outputs it should regulate — the metabolic system can occupy two self-reinforcing stable states under appropriate parameter conditions, transitions between them are nonlinear and threshold-dependent, the brakes that should oppose disease degrade with the fuel that runs them, and chronic disease in the bulk of cases is the durable settling of the system into the failure attractor.

If this sentence is true, the clinical translation follows: restore mitochondrial respiratory capacity as the prerequisite that enables every other regulatory mechanism to recover; intervene with biological multi-loop scope rather than mechanistically narrow targeting; reset slow variables (PUFA tissue burden, mitochondrial machinery integrity) for durability; intervene early before structural ceilings are crossed; discipline the daily oscillation between attractors; and stop treating chronic disease as a collection of independent pathologies that share a metabolic signature when the signature is the engine.

If the sentence is false at the existence layer, the framework collapses and the conventional picture of chronic disease holds. If it is false at the disease-dynamics layer, the architectural cascade weakens but the molecular mechanisms remain real and their per-disease clinical implications remain. If it is false at the universal-architecture layer, the framework's scope shrinks but the existence and disease-dynamics layers survive within their established domains.

The bet is asymmetric. **Active outputs is empirically secure.** State-dependent damping is high-confidence inference from textbook components plus the active-outputs principle. The clinical translation has been independently validated by two pharmaceutical R&D programs that arrived at metabolic interventions reversing the most common chronic liver disease through mechanisms at opposite ends of the predicted architecture, with no theoretical alignment to this framework.

This is what we now hold. The argument has been made. The disconfirmation criteria are specific. The position can be revised by evidence that meets them.

Until then, the most foundational answer available to the question of what health and disease are at the deepest resolution biology currently allows is this:

**Health is efficient oxidative metabolism, sustained by active outputs that maintain their own producing state and the regulatory machinery that supports them. Disease is its progressive failure, sustained by active outputs that maintain the failure state and degrade the regulatory machinery that should oppose it.**

A large fraction of what we know about enabling health and preventing or reversing chronic disease in primary-driver and central-pathway modes follows from this. The peripheral category (cystic fibrosis, Marfan, syndromic neurodevelopmental conditions) requires different first principles, and per-disease verification remains the appropriate empirical work for extending the universal-architecture claim into new domains.
