# Vault Stress Test

**Purpose:** Systematic attempt to break the metabolic foundations vault by attacking its most load-bearing claims first. If the foundation holds, descend. If the foundation breaks, stop and characterize exactly what failed.

**Orientation:** The object is truth, not vault-preservation. The vault is the current best approximation of what health actually is, derived from first principles. A successful break means we improve the vault. A failed break means we trust the vault more. Both outcomes are wins for truth-seeking.

**Dual stance — held consciously throughout:**
- **As stress-tester:** adversarial. Actively trying to break claims. Not assuming the vault is correct. The probe is free.
- **As change-gatekeeper:** if a break appears, the full six-step verification sequence runs before any proposed vault change. The change is expensive.

The methodology's caution is about changes to the vault, not about the right to probe hard. Probe with full adversarial intent; gate changes with full rigor.

**Ground rule:** Every test follows `metabolic_foundations/Verification Methodology.md` — see the Protocol section below. Apparent contradictions are more often integration-illiteracy than vault errors; deep reading of connected claims comes before declaring a break. The bar for calling a break is high, but the willingness to call one (when the bar is cleared) is absolute.

---

## Strategy

Attack bedrock first. Testing surface claims when the foundation might be wrong wastes effort. The logical dependency structure of the vault:

- If **Layer 1** fails, nothing above matters — the vault is a category error.
- If **Layer 2** fails, the attractor architecture collapses to linear metabolism.
- If **Layer 3** fails, "two stable states" becomes metaphor not dynamics.
- If **Layer 4** fails, the cross-disease claim overreaches.
- If **Layer 5** fails, the "integration is the missing step" story is wrong.

Layers 1–3 map directly onto the framework's pre-specified **Core Claims** (per `Verification Methodology.md` § The Core Claims):

- Layer 1 = **Core Claim 1** (health IS oxidative metabolism of glucose to CO₂)
- Layer 2 = the mechanism under **Core Claim 3** (positive feedback loops make each state self-maintaining)
- Layer 3 = **Core Claims 2 and 3** (two self-reinforcing stable states; bistability from interlocking reinforcement)

We are therefore attacking what the framework itself pre-identifies as core. If a core claim falls, the framework is falsified by its own declaration — no hiding behind peripheral modifications.

Each layer that holds, descend. Each layer that breaks, stop and characterize.

## Protocol for Each Layer Test

Every layer test follows `metabolic_foundations/Verification Methodology.md`. Specifically:

### The Three Hypotheses, In Order

When an apparent contradiction surfaces during a layer test, check these hypotheses in this sequence. Do not skip to hypothesis 3.

1. **I am misunderstanding the vault.** Most common and most dangerous because it feels like understanding. Deep reading of the connected claims comes first. Every term in the vault carries implications from its connections — "metabolic rate" does not mean raw throughput; "estrogen amplifies disease" does not mean it is categorically harmful.
2. **The research measures something different from what the vault is talking about.** Proxy endpoints (HbA1c, body weight, serum lipids, inflammatory markers) cannot confirm or deny claims about mitochondrial function — regardless of outcome.
3. **The vault is wrong.** Rarest case. High bar: specific, replicated, human-confirmed, direct measurement of the variables the claim operates on, surviving a deep reading of the vault's logic.

### The Six-Step Verification Sequence

Applied to each attack vector:

**Step 1. Deeply understand what the vault predicts.** Trace the full reasoning chain through wikilinks and System Connections tables. Two sub-practices are critical:

- *Unpack the prediction chain link by link.* Decompose each claim into constituent links and mark each as **textbook / well-supported / contested / inferred**. Concentrate verification effort on contested and inferred links. Don't waste effort attacking textbook bedrock.
- *List adjacent measurements before searching.* Don't anchor on the vault's vocabulary. For "CO₂ production," list V'CO2 (indirect calorimetry, the gold standard), ETCO₂ (capnography), BMR, RQ, resting energy expenditure — not just the vault's term. The strongest disconfirming evidence often lives in adjacent terminology.

**Step 2. Check the framework's own metrics.** V'CO2 / ETCO₂, blood lactate, RQ, body temperature, resting pulse, mitochondrial function markers (Complex IV activity, cardiolipin composition, coupling efficiency). What do these predict for this case? What do actual studies measure? Which metrics have NOT been measured? (This step often reveals that the relevant variables have never been assessed — meaning the apparent contradiction cannot be confirmed either way.)

**Step 3. State the contradiction precisely — if one still exists.** Many apparent contradictions dissolve after Steps 1–2. If one survives: state the exact vault claim, the exact evidence, and why they cannot be reconciled.

**Step 4. Identify what a resolution would depend on.** Write down every load-bearing claim the resolution requires. Each is a dependency. If any one collapses, the resolution collapses.

**Step 5. Verify each dependency against primary sources.** Replication? Human confirmation? Caveats (dose, duration, population, comparison, endpoint)? What was NOT measured? Apply the same rigor to supporting evidence as to challenging evidence — the evidence that conveniently confirms what we want to conclude needs the hardest pull. Distinguish citation accuracy from claim accuracy: a weak citation does not necessarily mean a weak claim.

**Step 6. Construct and stress-test the resolution.** What is the weakest link? Am I being more generous to this resolution than I would be to someone else's? Does it make the framework more precise (refinement) or more flexible (failure)? What new predictions does it generate? If none, it may be accommodation disguised as refinement.

### The Three Rules (Applied Throughout)

Rules from Verification Methodology that constrain when the framework's explanatory resources can be invoked:

**Rule 1 — Acute/Chronic as Control Architecture.** The acute/chronic distinction is valid only when **controlled deployment** is demonstrated via all four criteria: (a) entered under programmatic biological control, (b) built-in termination mechanism intact and will fire, (c) damage within repair/replacement capacity within the recovery window, (d) system returns to baseline or better. Cannot classify by outcome — that's circular and invalidates the resolution.

**Rule 2 — Context-Dependence.** Context-dependence is a valid resolution only when **all three** conditions are met: (a) the context variable is specified in advance, (b) it is independently measurable, (c) the prediction is directional and falsifiable. If any condition fails, the resolution is invalid and the case stands as an unresolved challenge. *This is what the original Layer 6 falsifiability meta-test was trying to capture — Rule 2 handles it more rigorously.*

**Rule 3 — Pass / Refinement / Fail.** Every resolution receives one of three verdicts:
- **Pass** — existing architecture handles the case with no modifications.
- **Refinement** — modification constrains the framework's claims (narrows predictions, increases specificity), generates new testable predictions, core claims survive.
- **Fail** — modification widens what the framework can explain, does not generate new predictions, forces a core claim to be abandoned, or reverses practical recommendations.

**Decisive test:** After resolution, can the framework predict more specific things than before, or fewer? Refinements increase specificity. Failures decrease it.

### Research Conducted Directly

Per the methodology: primary research and synthesis stay with the person holding the full framework context. Sub-agents without context cannot evaluate which findings are load-bearing — they don't hold the vault's logic in working memory. Sub-agents remain useful for **narrow retrieval tasks only** (latest meta-analysis on X, has study Y been replicated, IC50 of compound Z) — not for evaluation or synthesis.

## Layer 1 — The Ontological Move

**The claim:**
> Health IS efficient oxidative metabolism of glucose to CO₂. Disease IS its progressive failure.

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is where the vault commits itself to a single-variable answer — the move from bedrock biochemistry to an ontological identification. If health is not actually reducible to this one variable, every subsequent claim is elaborate gloss on a category error, no matter how correct the details.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Is it really GLUCOSE specifically?** The vault's argument (RQ 1.0 vs 0.7, Randle cycle) treats glucose as categorically superior to fat as fuel. But fat oxidation also produces ATP. Where's the threshold at which glucose-dominant becomes *required* rather than *preferable*? Can a ketogenic organism be healthy by the vault's own definition?

2. **Is CO₂ the endpoint that actually matters, or incidental?** If the vault claims CO₂ itself is load-bearing (Bohr effect, carbamino protection, pH-mediated mediator sequestration) rather than just being "what ATP production happens to produce," that's a stronger commitment worth isolating from the ATP claim and testing separately.

3. **Can health exist with low mitochondrial function?** Red blood cells have none. Certain cells are obligate glycolysers. What level of the hierarchy is the claim pitched at — cells, tissues, organisms? Does the claim admit exceptions by tissue type, or is it absolute?

4. **Do non-metabolic diseases actually operate "through" metabolic capacity?** Genetic diseases, mechanical trauma, infectious diseases, certain psychiatric conditions, autoimmune with clear antigen triggers. The vault's answer is "all operate through metabolic capacity." Is this a substantive claim or an unfalsifiable universal solvent?

5. **What does "efficient" mean quantitatively?** Inefficient oxidation still produces ATP. Where's the threshold between healthy and diseased in actual measurable terms? Can the vault specify it?

### Sub-test 1.B — Glucose Specificity via the CO₂ Argument

**Executed:** 2026-04-10
**Classification:** REFINEMENT (substantial, multi-component)

Triangulated via our own research + two independent agent analyses (`agent_respone_1.md`, `agent_response_2.md`). Both agents independently classified as REFINEMENT with convergent reasoning. The vault's core ontological commitment (efficient oxidative metabolism producing CO₂ via Krebs cycle is foundational to health) survives. Its quantitative and mechanism-specific arguments for glucose specificity need substantial tightening.

**R1.B.1 — The "40% more CO₂" quantitative claim is overstated.** Theoretical maximum at pure substrate endpoints, never achieved in humans. [Talpers et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1643946/) predict only ~22% V'CO₂ reduction even at theoretical complete isocaloric swap. Empirically, V'CO₂ at isocaloric matched conditions in ventilated patients does not differ significantly across 40%–75% CHO (205/203/211 ml/min). Real-world adapted keto shows ~2–15% differential depending on adaptation duration.

**R1.B.2 — Ketone bodies are a biochemically distinct third fuel category.** Modern ATP yields (P/O 2.5 NADH, 1.5 FADH₂) give: glucose ~32 ATP → CO₂/ATP ≈ **0.188**; palmitate ~106 ATP → **0.151**; β-hydroxybutyrate ~21.5 ATP → **0.186**; acetoacetate ~19 ATP → **0.211**. **BHB is essentially equivalent to glucose in CO₂/ATP; acetoacetate exceeds glucose; palmitate is clearly lower.** The vault's "fat oxidation produces less CO₂" framing is accurate only for raw long-chain fatty acid β-oxidation as final pathway (unfed fasting, Randle pathology) and NOT for adapted ketogenic physiology where peripheral tissues oxidize ketone bodies via the Krebs cycle at CO₂/ATP ratios matching or exceeding glucose.

**R1.B.3 — Randle pathology ≠ adapted ketosis.** Randle pathology requires TWO conditions simultaneously: (i) FFA excess inhibiting PDH AND (ii) glucose availability providing pyruvate to divert. Adapted keto has (i) but not (ii), so no pyruvate-to-lactate shunting occurs. Resting lactate in adapted keto is typically normal or low, directly unlike Randle pathology. The vault currently collapses two biochemically distinct states under "fat oxidation" and applies Randle-pathology concerns to both.

**R1.B.4 — Cerebral perfusion prediction is empirically refuted for dietary keto.** [BHB infusion](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0190556) increases cerebral blood flow +27% (40.9 → 52.0 ml/100g/min) with arterial CO₂ and pH unchanged; a 3-week ketogenic diet in healthy adults increases global CBF +22% and BDNF +47%. Authors explicitly state the CBF increase "could not be explained by alterations in CO₂ tension or pH." The vault's CO₂ vasoreactivity physiology is real but does not dominate under dietary keto adaptation. **Caveat (Agent 2):** high-dose exogenous ketone monoester studies show CBF CAN decrease along with ETCO₂ — context-dependent, so CO₂ vasoreactivity still matters in specific interventions.

**R1.B.5 — Hepatic ketogenesis shunt is mechanistically validated but clinically inverted.** [PNAS 2020 isotope tracer study](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1922344117) confirms that on 6-day ketogenic diet, hepatic mitochondrial redox state increases +167%, inhibiting citrate synthase, diverting acetyl-CoA from Krebs toward ketogenesis. Local liver CO₂ production decreases. But this same mechanism drives NAFLD reversal in 6 days, with improvements in hepatic steatosis and insulin sensitivity. The vault's concern about hepatic CO₂ is mechanistically real; the implication that reduced hepatic CO₂ is universally pathological is inverted in the NAFLD population where the shunt is the recovery mechanism.

**R1.B.6 — Free T3 reduction (−21%, Luukkonen 2020) on 6-day keto is the deepest unresolved question.** Adaptive vs pathological is genuinely contested. Direct measurement of tissue-level T3 signaling (receptor occupancy, downstream gene expression) has not been performed in adapted keto humans. This is the remaining open question where the vault's concerns may or may not hold — and the highest-value single measurement for resolving it.

**Proposed vault edits (both agents converge on essentially the same prescription):**
- Distinguish **Randle pathology** from **adapted ketosis** explicitly in the main text of `The Preferred Substrate.md` and `The Metabolic Fork.md` — these are biochemically distinct states the vault currently conflates
- Reframe Core Claim 1 to emphasize "efficient mitochondrial oxidative metabolism producing CO₂ via the Krebs cycle" as the invariant, with **glucose as preferred substrate under normal conditions** rather than as the sole/exclusive foundational substrate
- Add ketone bodies as a **third fuel category** distinct from glucose and raw fat in the vault's fuel taxonomy
- Correct the "40% more CO₂" claim to realistic values with Talpers quantitative context
- Update cerebral perfusion claims to reflect that CBF can rise on dietary keto via non-CO₂ mechanisms, with the exogenous ketone monoester nuance preserved
- Migrate "long-term keto unresolved" from warning callouts into main text
- Flag Free T3 reduction as the deepest remaining unresolved question for future measurement

**Candidate subtlety preserved (Agent 1's observation):** Glucose oxidation produces 2/6 CO₂ from PDH upstream of Krebs; ketone body oxidation produces 100% of its CO₂ from Krebs enzymes only. The CO₂ molecules themselves are biochemically identical, but the subcellular location of production differs. Whether this yields meaningful microdomain effects (local pH gradients, carbamino formation, compartment-specific signaling) is a deeper biochemical question not probed in current literature. Could, in principle, preserve a "glucose specifically" claim at a level not currently tested. Not enough evidence to change the classification but worth flagging as a future research avenue.

## Layer 2 — The Product-Activity Claim

**The claim:**
> CO₂ and lactate are not inert products. Each is active in ways that reinforce the production of more of itself.

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is what generates the positive feedback that creates the attractor architecture. Without it, you have linear metabolism, not bistability. No self-reinforcing states. The whole "two attractors" framing rests on each product being an active participant in determining which fork the next round of metabolism takes.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Is the Bohr effect quantitatively large enough in vivo to meaningfully create "more oxidation sustains more oxidation"?** Real effect, but possibly too small to be structural. How much does O₂ delivery actually shift under realistic physiological CO₂ swings?

2. **Is histone lactylation (Zhang 2019) primary or marker-level?** Published finding, rapidly growing field. But is lactate actually *driving* gene expression change, or *reflecting* it? Magnitude vs. causation — what's the actual weight of evidence?

3. **Is carbamino protein protection clinically significant in vivo?** Real chemistry. But does it operate at physiologically meaningful magnitudes, or is it niche chemistry that rarely reaches clinical weight?

4. **Are there counter-mechanisms (negative feedback) the vault under-weights that would prevent positive-feedback runaway?** Bicarbonate buffering, HIF-1α degradation, lactate clearance pathways — is the vault cherry-picking positive-feedback components and under-counting the damping?

### Sub-test 2.A — Loop 7 (CO₂-Serotonin Loop) Mechanism

**Executed:** 2026-04-10
**Classification:** REFINEMENT (substantial, mechanism-level), with explicit structural consequence for Layer 2's broader "CO₂ is an active product" claim

Step 1 chain unpacking identified that **Loop 7 is the only formalized loop in the 13-loop architecture where CO₂ is the active driving mechanism**. Other CO₂ claims (Bohr, carbamino protection, Bolevich & Kogan mitochondrial ROS suppression, neuroprotection, CBF regulation) are mentioned in the attractor narrative as supporting protective mechanisms but are NOT formalized as numbered positive feedback loops. Layer 2's "CO₂ is an active product" claim therefore rests structurally on Loop 7 holding as a real positive feedback loop with CO₂ as the causal driver.

Sub-test 2.A was triangulated via own research + two independent agent analyses (`agent_1_report.md`, `agent_2_report.md`). Both agents independently converged on REFINEMENT with **identical** rescue mechanism: ATP → V-ATPase activity → granule pH → mediator release, with CO₂ as a parallel marker of oxidative metabolism rather than the causal driver. This level of independent convergence via matching first-principles reasoning is strong triangulation signal.

**R2.A.1 — The vault's stated mechanism is first-principles incompatible with V-ATPase biology.** Loop 7 claims extracellular CO₂ → carbonic acid → acidic pH → granule interior pH → mediator sequestration. Verified: mast cell secretory granules and platelet dense granules maintain interior pH ~5.3-5.5 via V-ATPase proton pumping against a ~1.9-unit gradient from cytosol (~7.2). [Nunomura et al. 2017](https://www.nature.com/articles/cddis2017206) shows bafilomycin A1 (V-ATPase inhibitor) is *required* to raise granule pH enough to reduce intracellular histamine and increase extracellular histamine — meaning extracellular perturbations alone don't propagate. [Poëa-Guyon et al. 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24165939/) (verified directly) demonstrates the V-ATPase V₀ membrane domain is the intragranule pH sensor, with V₁-V₀ dissociation correlating with exocytosis readiness — the sensing operates on the granule lumen side, not extracellularly. Physiological extracellular pH swings (~0.05-0.15 units from PaCO₂ variation) traversing plasma membrane active regulators (NHE1, AE3), then cytosol, then the 1.9-unit V-ATPase-maintained granule gradient, cannot deliver meaningful pH change into the granule interior. The mechanism as stated is biochemically implausible.

**R2.A.2 — Both vault citations for Loop 7 are mis-deployed.**
- [Pollard et al. 1977](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC431691/): in vitro isolated human platelets stimulated by thrombin or Ca²⁺ ionophore A23187. Proposed mechanism is OH⁻ transport + osmotic lysis of the granule-plasma membrane complex, operating only *during* stimulated exocytosis when granules are already juxtaposed to the plasma membrane. The paper does not test spontaneous release at physiological pH swings and does not support the vault's "CO₂ maintains granule pH at rest" claim.
- [Freed et al. 2001](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11549533/): canine model with cool-dry-air hyperventilation measuring bronchial reactivity to *exogenous* intravenous histamine (not endogenous release). The authors explicitly attribute findings to airway drying/osmolality at local airway mast cells, not systemic CO₂/pH effects. Clear citation-vs-claim gap — the paper's species, measurement, and proposed mechanism all differ from what the vault uses it to support.

**R2.A.3 — Other CO₂-mast-cell literature does not rescue the stated mechanism.**
- [Strider et al. 2011](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21284650/) (Allergy): 100% CO₂ pretreatment suppresses rat peritoneal mast cell degranulation >95% via >70% repression of intracellular calcium. But 100% CO₂ is ~20x physiological alveolar CO₂; the mechanism is calcium, not pH; no replication or physiological-range dose-response exists 14+ years later. [Casale et al. 2008](https://www.jacionline.org/article/S0091-6749(07)01657-0/fulltext) confirms the same mechanism has clinical effect (intranasal 100% CO₂ for allergic rhinitis) but remains at supraphysiological dose. Both confirm CO₂ can modulate mast cells at pharmacological extremes; neither demonstrates physiological-range effects.
- [Barnes & Brown 1981](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7261540/): isocapnic voluntary hyperventilation produces no plasma histamine change in healthy or asthmatic humans. Rules out the mechanical-hyperventilation arm as a driver. Does not test hypocapnic hyperventilation directly (isocapnic design deliberately controls CO₂ out).
- [Fujii et al. 2012](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22972836/): voluntary hypocapnic hyperventilation (ETCO₂ −23 to −25 torr) in heated humans *reduced* forearm cutaneous blood flow. Consistent with "mediator release does not dominate hypocapnic response at cutaneous sites" but no mediators measured directly; subjects under thermal stress.
- [Möllerherm et al. 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28553287/): 1% O₂ hypoxia increases mast cell histamine release under *S. aureus* stimulation. Consistent with ATP-mediated mechanism (hypoxia → reduced OXPHOS → reduced ATP → V-ATPase impaired → histamine release). Supports the ATP rescue.

**R2.A.4 — The refined mechanism is biochemically deeper than the original.** Replace "extracellular CO₂ → granule pH" with **"cytosolic ATP → V-ATPase activity → granule pH → V₁-V₀ dissociation sensor → mediator release"**. Mast cells and platelets have a built-in ATP-to-exocytosis coupling at the granule level: V-ATPase pumps H⁺ using cytosolic ATP, and its V₀ membrane domain simultaneously senses interior pH via V₁-V₀ dissociation. When cellular energetics fall, pumping slows, granule pH rises, V₁ dissociates, and the dissociation couples to exocytosis readiness. This is *more* supportive of the vault's deeper thesis (that metabolic state directly controls mediator release) than the original mechanism — the coupling is tighter, built into the cell architecture, and biochemically complete. The vault's intuition was right; the chemistry it wrote down was wrong; the chemistry that's actually there is more supportive of the intuition than what it wrote.

**R2.A.5 — Loop gain > 1 remains unmeasured at physiological ATP variations.** The refined mechanism has only been demonstrated at pharmacological extremes — bafilomycin (full V-ATPase inhibition), 1% O₂ (severe hypoxia), 100% CO₂ (supraphysiological ceiling). No experiment has measured granule pH or mediator release as a function of graded mild-to-moderate ATP depletion matching physiological metabolic variation (±20-40%). The refined loop is a mechanistically plausible directional coupling; whether it has sufficient loop gain to generate genuine bistability (vs. merely a damped directional response) is unknown. This applies to both the original and refined formulations.

**R2.A.6 — The refinement forces a structural rebalancing of Layer 2's broader "CO₂ is an active product" claim.** After the refinement, CO₂ is no longer the causal variable in Loop 7 — ATP is. This means Loop 7 no longer supports the vault's claim that "CO₂ is an active product" at the formal architecture level. The claim must be reframed onto its non-loop active mechanisms: the Bohr effect on tissue O₂ delivery (real, textbook, directionally reinforcing but not formalized as a loop), carbamino protein protection (mechanism plausible, protective magnitude never directly tested — vault already flags this), Bolevich & Kogan mitochondrial ROS suppression (single-study, not re-verified in this sub-test — see CF-2.A.5), and neuroprotection (local brain effect via NMDA modulation). These are all real mechanisms but none is a formalized positive feedback loop in the 13-loop architecture. The vault's Layer 2 claim survives in the sense that CO₂ has real biological effects, but it loses its sole formalized structural support in the attractor framework and must be explicitly reframed to avoid over-claiming.

## Layer 3 — Attractor Architecture as Genuine Dynamics

**The claim:**
> The system has two stable states (attractors) maintained by 13 interlocking positive feedback loops in 5 architectural layers. Transition between states requires threshold crossing via simultaneous multi-loop perturbation.

**Why it's load-bearing:** "Attractor" is a rigorous concept in dynamical systems. If the metabolic system doesn't actually exhibit bistability as formal dynamics, the entire two-state framing collapses to a metaphor for clinical bimodality rather than a description of system structure. The explanatory power of "why single interventions fail," "why disease is progressive," "why tipping appears sudden" all rest on this being real dynamics, not metaphor.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Has this been formally modeled as a dynamical system?** Either the math shows bistability for parameter values in physiological ranges, or "attractor" is functioning as a precise metaphor. Both can be true in some sense but they're different claims.

2. **Where's the unstable equilibrium?** Bistable systems characteristically exhibit hysteresis — the system's state depends on its history, not just current inputs. Is this observed?

3. **Do clinical populations show bimodal distributions of key variables (ETCO₂, lactate, temperature, T3)?** Two attractors predict bimodality in the right variables. Continuous distributions would suggest a spectrum, not bistability.

4. **Are the 13 loops independently supported at the claimed strengths?** The count is architecturally impressive, but individual loop robustness matters. Which loops have the strongest evidence and which are the weakest links?

5. **Does breaking one loop actually propagate the way the architecture predicts?** Interventions that target single loops — is the "plateau after initial improvement" pattern actually observed, and does it match the model's quantitative predictions?

### 2026-04-11 — Causal-independence audit tool (derived from Sub-test 2.A's deeper observation)

The deeper structural observation from Sub-test 2.A — that CO₂ and ATP are stoichiometrically co-produced by OXPHOS in fixed coupling and therefore CO₂ cannot in principle be an independent feedback participant in any loop whose downstream effect feeds back into OXPHOS — was promoted from a one-loop observation to an audit tool intended to span the full 13-loop architecture.

- **First pass (drafted, then superseded):** Generalized as a "Co-Production / Independence Principle" in a standalone refinement document (`Co-Production and Independence.md`). Stated as a universal rule: *a metabolite can act as an independent causal participant in a positive feedback loop only if its production is not stoichiometrically tied to the same upstream process the loop is supposed to influence.* Intended to be added directly to Verification Methodology as a new audit step.

- **Triangulation round (2026-04-11):** Before adding the principle to the methodology, it was stress-tested via two independent general-purpose agents per `Independent Agent Research Protocol.md`. A self-contained research brief (`Research Brief - Co-Production Principle.md`, since deleted) was constructed with the principle, reasoning chain, first-principles biochemistry, the Sub-test 2.A findings, and the 13-loop architecture. The brief was written neutrally — the agents were explicitly instructed not to read other files in the vault, to reason from first principles, and to check whether the concept already exists in established literature.

- **Both agents independently classified the first-pass principle as REFINEMENT** and converged on three critical problems:
  1. **The concept is not novel.** Both agents independently identified it as a partial re-derivation of conservation relations in Metabolic Control Analysis (Reder 1988; Heinrich & Schuster 1996; Palsson 2003 on the left null space of the stoichiometric matrix; Kacser & Burns 1973 and Heinrich & Rapoport 1974 on flux/concentration control coefficients) and Pearl's do-calculus / screening-off test (Pearl 2009 *Causality*). The right move: ground the audit in these existing frameworks with explicit citations, not propose it as a new primitive.
  2. **The universal "only if" form is falsified by at least two counterexamples.**
     - **Soluble adenylyl cyclase (sAC)** — Agent 1's strongest find. sAC is bicarbonate-activated at a specific allosteric site (Arg176; salt bridge disruption; active-site closure — [Kleinboelting et al. 2014 *PNAS*](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1322778111) crystal structures) and regulates OXPHOS via PKA phosphorylation of Complex I NDUFS4 and Complex IV COX subunit IV-1 ([Acin-Perez et al. 2009 *Cell Metabolism*](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2814779/); [Valsecchi et al. 2014 *BBA*](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4257896/)). ATP functions as substrate, not activator. The first-pass principle would misclassify CO₂/bicarbonate here as parallel marker; biochemistry says it is an independent causal variable. **Verified against primary sources via WebSearch/WebFetch on 2026-04-11.** Caveat (per Valsecchi 2014): the pathway is framed in the primary literature as feedforward metabolic matching rather than closed positive feedback, which narrows the counterexample's directness (the framework does not currently formalize an sAC-based loop, so the first-pass principle's misclassification is hypothetical within the architecture) but preserves its core structural point — stoichiometric coupling alone does not disqualify a metabolite from independent causal status.
     - **AMP/AMPK** — both agents independently. AMP is in the conserved adenylate pool with ATP (2 ADP ⇌ ATP + AMP via adenylate kinase) but is a genuine causal participant because AMPK γ-subunit CBS domains are AMP-specific (Gowans, Hawley, Ross, Hardie 2013 *Cell Metabolism*; Hardie 2012 *Nature Reviews Molecular Cell Biology*). The adenylate kinase equilibrium gives AMP a ~quadratic amplification relative to ATP changes, making AMP a more sensitive signal than ATP itself.
  3. **The correct operational criterion is Pearl's screening-off test, not stoichiometric coupling per se.** Right question: "Can the metabolite be varied independently of any stoichiometrically co-produced partner through a physiologically realizable intervention, and does it act on the downstream target through a specific molecular mechanism distinct from the partner's mechanism?" Stoichiometric coupling is a diagnostic flag that triggers the screening-off check, not a definition of parallel-marker status.

- **Rewrite and rename (2026-04-11):** The first-pass document (`Co-Production and Independence.md`) was deleted and replaced by `Causal Independence in Feedback Loops.md`, which:
  - Grounds the audit in the existing frameworks with explicit citations (Pearl 2009; Reder 1988; Heinrich & Schuster 1996; Palsson 2003; Kacser & Burns 1973; Heinrich & Rapoport 1974)
  - Replaces the universal rule with a four-check operational audit (conservation / mechanism specificity / realizable intervention / quantitative dominance)
