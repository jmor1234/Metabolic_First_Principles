## Priority 3: Layer 4 — Cross-disease convergence

Tests the framework's strongest external prediction: every major chronic disease shows the same metabolic failure signature (elevated lactate, shifted RQ, mitochondrial dysfunction, impaired oxidative capacity).

**Why it matters:** If the convergence holds, the thesis ("health IS oxidative metabolism, disease IS its failure") has independent cross-specialty confirmation. If it doesn't hold universally, the thesis is overclaimed — and the practical implication changes from "all chronic disease responds to the same metabolic approach" to "some chronic disease responds to this approach."

**Attack vectors:**
1. Universal convergence or disease-specific exceptions? Autoimmune conditions with clear antigen triggers, monogenic diseases, neurodevelopmental conditions — do they show the metabolic signature?
2. "Same signature" clean or ad-hoc reinterpretation? Does every observation get retroactively explained to fit, or are there genuine a priori commitments?
3. Metabolic failure upstream or downstream? Even if the signature is real across diseases, is it cause or consequence? Correlation vs causation at the cross-disease level.

**What "done" looks like:** A systematic probe of 5-8 chronic conditions (including at least 2 that challenge the thesis — e.g., autoimmune with identified antigen, monogenic disease, neurodevelopmental) testing whether the metabolic failure signature appears, whether it's upstream or downstream, and whether the framework's architecture predicts the specific presentation. Findings classified per the stress test methodology (pass / refinement / fail).

**Status:** NOT STARTED

## Priority 4: Species qualifiers and causal overattributions

Honesty work that prevents the framework from being more confident than the evidence supports.

**Species qualifiers (~12 citations):** Animal data (rat, mouse, canine) presented as if directly applicable to humans without noting the species gap. These need explicit qualifiers: "demonstrated in rat peritoneal mast cells" rather than "PTH triggers mast cell degranulation." The mechanism may translate to humans, but the evidence is from another species and the reader should know.

Distributed across multiple documents; highest concentration in Conservation Signaling, The Cellular Readout, and The Gut-Liver Axis.

**Causal overattributions (~6 citations):** Claims that attribute causal mechanisms beyond what the cited paper's design can support. The stress test's error pattern: "citation deployment errors cluster where mechanisms are named at the bulk-effect level rather than the molecular level." These need either stronger citations that support the specific causal claim, or reframing to match what the evidence actually shows.

**What "done" looks like:** Every animal-data citation has a species qualifier. Every causal attribution is supported by evidence at the level claimed, or reframed to the level the evidence supports.

**Status:** NOT STARTED

## Priority 5: Layer 5 — Epistemic meta-claim

Tests the framework's explanation for why this integration doesn't exist in mainstream medicine: "the evidence already exists, scattered across specialties that don't cite each other."

**Why it matters:** Systems biology, systems medicine, and integrative physiology have existed as academic subfields for decades. If metabolism-centric unified theories have been proposed, evaluated, and rejected on substantive grounds, the framework should engage with those objections — not attribute the gap entirely to disciplinary fragmentation and financial architecture.

**Attack vectors:**
1. Have others attempted this integration? What did they conclude? Were they rejected on substantive scientific grounds the framework doesn't address?
2. When specialists (cardiologists, endocrinologists, oncologists) are presented with metabolism-centric unified theories, what are their specific counter-arguments? Does the framework steelman them?

**What "done" looks like:** A survey of existing metabolism-centric integrative theories in the academic literature, identification of the strongest substantive objections to the approach, and honest assessment of whether the framework engages with or papers over those objections. If substantive objections exist that the framework doesn't address, they should be documented and either resolved or acknowledged as genuine limitations.

**Status:** NOT STARTED
