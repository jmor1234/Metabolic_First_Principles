# Vault Stress Test

**Purpose:** Systematic attempt to break the metabolic foundations vault by attacking its most load-bearing claims first. If the foundation holds, descend. If the foundation breaks, stop and characterize exactly what failed.

**Orientation:** The object is truth, not vault-preservation. The vault is the current best approximation of what health actually is, derived from first principles. A successful break means we improve the vault. A failed break means we trust the vault more. Both outcomes are wins for truth-seeking.

**Dual stance — held consciously throughout:**
- **As stress-tester:** adversarial. Actively trying to break claims. Not assuming the vault is correct. The probe is free.
- **As change-gatekeeper:** if a break appears, the full six-step verification sequence runs before any proposed vault change. The change is expensive.

The methodology's caution is about changes to the vault, not about the right to probe hard. Probe with full adversarial intent; gate changes with full rigor.

**Ground rule:** Every test follows `metabolic_foundations/Verification Methodology.md` — see the Protocol section below. Apparent contradictions are more often integration-illiteracy than vault errors; deep reading of connected claims comes before declaring a break. The bar for calling a break is high, but the willingness to call one (when the bar is cleared) is absolute.

---

## Strategy

Attack bedrock first. Testing surface claims when the foundation might be wrong wastes effort. The logical dependency structure of the vault:

- If **Layer 1** fails, nothing above matters — the vault is a category error.
- If **Layer 2** fails, the attractor architecture collapses to linear metabolism.
- If **Layer 3** fails, "two stable states" becomes metaphor not dynamics.
- If **Layer 4** fails, the cross-disease claim overreaches.
- If **Layer 5** fails, the "integration is the missing step" story is wrong.

Layers 1–3 map directly onto the framework's pre-specified **Core Claims** (per `Verification Methodology.md` § The Core Claims):

- Layer 1 = **Core Claim 1** (health IS oxidative metabolism of glucose to CO₂)
- Layer 2 = the mechanism under **Core Claim 3** (positive feedback loops make each state self-maintaining)
- Layer 3 = **Core Claims 2 and 3** (two self-reinforcing stable states; bistability from interlocking reinforcement)

We are therefore attacking what the framework itself pre-identifies as core. If a core claim falls, the framework is falsified by its own declaration — no hiding behind peripheral modifications.

Each layer that holds, descend. Each layer that breaks, stop and characterize.

## Protocol for Each Layer Test

Every layer test follows `metabolic_foundations/Verification Methodology.md`. Specifically:

### The Three Hypotheses, In Order

When an apparent contradiction surfaces during a layer test, check these hypotheses in this sequence. Do not skip to hypothesis 3.

1. **I am misunderstanding the vault.** Most common and most dangerous because it feels like understanding. Deep reading of the connected claims comes first. Every term in the vault carries implications from its connections — "metabolic rate" does not mean raw throughput; "estrogen amplifies disease" does not mean it is categorically harmful.
2. **The research measures something different from what the vault is talking about.** Proxy endpoints (HbA1c, body weight, serum lipids, inflammatory markers) cannot confirm or deny claims about mitochondrial function — regardless of outcome.
3. **The vault is wrong.** Rarest case. High bar: specific, replicated, human-confirmed, direct measurement of the variables the claim operates on, surviving a deep reading of the vault's logic.

### The Six-Step Verification Sequence

Applied to each attack vector:

**Step 1. Deeply understand what the vault predicts.** Trace the full reasoning chain through wikilinks and System Connections tables. Two sub-practices are critical:

- *Unpack the prediction chain link by link.* Decompose each claim into constituent links and mark each as **textbook / well-supported / contested / inferred**. Concentrate verification effort on contested and inferred links. Don't waste effort attacking textbook bedrock.
- *List adjacent measurements before searching.* Don't anchor on the vault's vocabulary. For "CO₂ production," list V'CO2 (indirect calorimetry, the gold standard), ETCO₂ (capnography), BMR, RQ, resting energy expenditure — not just the vault's term. The strongest disconfirming evidence often lives in adjacent terminology.

**Step 2. Check the framework's own metrics.** V'CO2 / ETCO₂, blood lactate, RQ, body temperature, resting pulse, mitochondrial function markers (Complex IV activity, cardiolipin composition, coupling efficiency). What do these predict for this case? What do actual studies measure? Which metrics have NOT been measured? (This step often reveals that the relevant variables have never been assessed — meaning the apparent contradiction cannot be confirmed either way.)

**Step 3. State the contradiction precisely — if one still exists.** Many apparent contradictions dissolve after Steps 1–2. If one survives: state the exact vault claim, the exact evidence, and why they cannot be reconciled.

**Step 4. Identify what a resolution would depend on.** Write down every load-bearing claim the resolution requires. Each is a dependency. If any one collapses, the resolution collapses.

**Step 5. Verify each dependency against primary sources.** Replication? Human confirmation? Caveats (dose, duration, population, comparison, endpoint)? What was NOT measured? Apply the same rigor to supporting evidence as to challenging evidence — the evidence that conveniently confirms what we want to conclude needs the hardest pull. Distinguish citation accuracy from claim accuracy: a weak citation does not necessarily mean a weak claim.

**Step 6. Construct and stress-test the resolution.** What is the weakest link? Am I being more generous to this resolution than I would be to someone else's? Does it make the framework more precise (refinement) or more flexible (failure)? What new predictions does it generate? If none, it may be accommodation disguised as refinement.

### The Three Rules (Applied Throughout)

Rules from Verification Methodology that constrain when the framework's explanatory resources can be invoked:

**Rule 1 — Acute/Chronic as Control Architecture.** The acute/chronic distinction is valid only when **controlled deployment** is demonstrated via all four criteria: (a) entered under programmatic biological control, (b) built-in termination mechanism intact and will fire, (c) damage within repair/replacement capacity within the recovery window, (d) system returns to baseline or better. Cannot classify by outcome — that's circular and invalidates the resolution.

**Rule 2 — Context-Dependence.** Context-dependence is a valid resolution only when **all three** conditions are met: (a) the context variable is specified in advance, (b) it is independently measurable, (c) the prediction is directional and falsifiable. If any condition fails, the resolution is invalid and the case stands as an unresolved challenge. *This is what the original Layer 6 falsifiability meta-test was trying to capture — Rule 2 handles it more rigorously.*

**Rule 3 — Pass / Refinement / Fail.** Every resolution receives one of three verdicts:
- **Pass** — existing architecture handles the case with no modifications.
- **Refinement** — modification constrains the framework's claims (narrows predictions, increases specificity), generates new testable predictions, core claims survive.
- **Fail** — modification widens what the framework can explain, does not generate new predictions, forces a core claim to be abandoned, or reverses practical recommendations.

**Decisive test:** After resolution, can the framework predict more specific things than before, or fewer? Refinements increase specificity. Failures decrease it.

### Research Conducted Directly

Per the methodology: primary research and synthesis stay with the person holding the full framework context. Sub-agents without context cannot evaluate which findings are load-bearing — they don't hold the vault's logic in working memory. Sub-agents remain useful for **narrow retrieval tasks only** (latest meta-analysis on X, has study Y been replicated, IC50 of compound Z) — not for evaluation or synthesis.

## Layer 0 — Bedrock (Not Tested)

Cells require ATP. Oxidative phosphorylation yields ~18× more ATP per glucose than glycolysis. Both pathways are real and textbook.

Attacking these would mean overturning mainstream biochemistry. If they fall, biology falls — not this vault. Not where effort belongs.

---

## Layer 1 — The Ontological Move

**The claim:**
> Health IS efficient oxidative metabolism of glucose to CO₂. Disease IS its progressive failure.

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is where the vault commits itself to a single-variable answer — the move from bedrock biochemistry to an ontological identification. If health is not actually reducible to this one variable, every subsequent claim is elaborate gloss on a category error, no matter how correct the details.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Is it really GLUCOSE specifically?** The vault's argument (RQ 1.0 vs 0.7, Randle cycle) treats glucose as categorically superior to fat as fuel. But fat oxidation also produces ATP. Where's the threshold at which glucose-dominant becomes *required* rather than *preferable*? Can a ketogenic organism be healthy by the vault's own definition?

2. **Is CO₂ the endpoint that actually matters, or incidental?** If the vault claims CO₂ itself is load-bearing (Bohr effect, carbamino protection, pH-mediated mediator sequestration) rather than just being "what ATP production happens to produce," that's a stronger commitment worth isolating from the ATP claim and testing separately.

3. **Can health exist with low mitochondrial function?** Red blood cells have none. Certain cells are obligate glycolysers. What level of the hierarchy is the claim pitched at — cells, tissues, organisms? Does the claim admit exceptions by tissue type, or is it absolute?

4. **Do non-metabolic diseases actually operate "through" metabolic capacity?** Genetic diseases, mechanical trauma, infectious diseases, certain psychiatric conditions, autoimmune with clear antigen triggers. The vault's answer is "all operate through metabolic capacity." Is this a substantive claim or an unfalsifiable universal solvent?

5. **What does "efficient" mean quantitatively?** Inefficient oxidation still produces ATP. Where's the threshold between healthy and diseased in actual measurable terms? Can the vault specify it?

## Layer 2 — The Product-Activity Claim

**The claim:**
> CO₂ and lactate are not inert products. Each is active in ways that reinforce the production of more of itself.

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is what generates the positive feedback that creates the attractor architecture. Without it, you have linear metabolism, not bistability. No self-reinforcing states. The whole "two attractors" framing rests on each product being an active participant in determining which fork the next round of metabolism takes.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Is the Bohr effect quantitatively large enough in vivo to meaningfully create "more oxidation sustains more oxidation"?** Real effect, but possibly too small to be structural. How much does O₂ delivery actually shift under realistic physiological CO₂ swings?

2. **Is histone lactylation (Zhang 2019) primary or marker-level?** Published finding, rapidly growing field. But is lactate actually *driving* gene expression change, or *reflecting* it? Magnitude vs. causation — what's the actual weight of evidence?

3. **Is carbamino protein protection clinically significant in vivo?** Real chemistry. But does it operate at physiologically meaningful magnitudes, or is it niche chemistry that rarely reaches clinical weight?

4. **Are there counter-mechanisms (negative feedback) the vault under-weights that would prevent positive-feedback runaway?** Bicarbonate buffering, HIF-1α degradation, lactate clearance pathways — is the vault cherry-picking positive-feedback components and under-counting the damping?

**Status:** NOT STARTED

## Layer 3 — Attractor Architecture as Genuine Dynamics

**The claim:**
> The system has two stable states (attractors) maintained by 13 interlocking positive feedback loops in 5 architectural layers. Transition between states requires threshold crossing via simultaneous multi-loop perturbation.

**Why it's load-bearing:** "Attractor" is a rigorous concept in dynamical systems. If the metabolic system doesn't actually exhibit bistability as formal dynamics, the entire two-state framing collapses to a metaphor for clinical bimodality rather than a description of system structure. The explanatory power of "why single interventions fail," "why disease is progressive," "why tipping appears sudden" all rest on this being real dynamics, not metaphor.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Has this been formally modeled as a dynamical system?** Either the math shows bistability for parameter values in physiological ranges, or "attractor" is functioning as a precise metaphor. Both can be true in some sense but they're different claims.

2. **Where's the unstable equilibrium?** Bistable systems characteristically exhibit hysteresis — the system's state depends on its history, not just current inputs. Is this observed?

3. **Do clinical populations show bimodal distributions of key variables (ETCO₂, lactate, temperature, T3)?** Two attractors predict bimodality in the right variables. Continuous distributions would suggest a spectrum, not bistability.

4. **Are the 13 loops independently supported at the claimed strengths?** The count is architecturally impressive, but individual loop robustness matters. Which loops have the strongest evidence and which are the weakest links?

5. **Does breaking one loop actually propagate the way the architecture predicts?** Interventions that target single loops — is the "plateau after initial improvement" pattern actually observed, and does it match the model's quantitative predictions?

## Layer 4 — Cross-Disease Convergence

**The claim:**
> Every major chronic disease shows the same metabolic failure signature (elevated lactate, shifted RQ, mitochondrial dysfunction, impaired oxidative capacity).

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is the strongest external prediction. If it holds, it's independent cross-specialty evidence for the unified-metabolism thesis. If it fails, the "single system, single failure mode" framing overreaches.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Is convergence universal, or are there chronic diseases that genuinely don't fit?** Autoimmune conditions with clear antigen triggers, monogenic diseases, neurodevelopmental conditions. Do they show the signature? If not, does the vault acknowledge them or paper over them?

2. **Is "same signature" clean, or does each disease require ad-hoc reinterpretation to fit?** A framework that explains every observation by reinterpreting each to fit has weaker predictive power than one that makes clean a priori commitments.

3. **Even if the signature is real, is metabolic failure upstream or downstream of something else?** Chronic inflammation, cellular senescence, accumulated DNA damage — alternative upstream candidates. Correlation ≠ causation. What specifically makes metabolism the upstream driver rather than a parallel or downstream process?

## Layer 5 — The Epistemic Meta-Claim

**The claim:**
> The integration is the missing step. Evidence already exists in specialty literature that doesn't cross-cite.

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is the vault's explanation for why this picture hasn't been assembled by mainstream medicine. If the integration has been attempted and rejected for substantive reasons the vault doesn't address, "disciplinary fragmentation" is an insufficient explanation and suggests the vault may be repeating errors specialists already considered and rejected.

**Attack vectors:**

1. **Have others attempted this integration and been rejected on substantive grounds?** Systems biology, systems medicine, integrative physiology as academic subfields have existed for decades. What do they conclude, and on what grounds have metabolism-centric unified theories been rejected (if they have)?

2. **Are there specialist objections the vault doesn't engage with?** Cardiologists, endocrinologists, oncologists — when presented with the integrated thesis, what are their specific counter-arguments? Does the vault steelman them?

## Layer 6 — Subsumed into Protocol

Originally planned as a methodological meta-test for falsifiability: the concern that if every disconfirming result can be re-explained as "wrong attractor state" or "context-dependence," the framework becomes unfalsifiable by design.

On reading `Verification Methodology.md`, this concern is already handled — more rigorously than the original Layer 6 plan — by **Rule 2** (context-dependence must be pre-specified, independently measurable, and generate directional falsifiable predictions) and **Rule 3** (pass/refinement/fail classification, where a resolution that widens what the framework explains without generating new predictions is a failure, not a refinement).

**No separate layer test.** Rules 1–3 run during every Layer 1–5 attack via the Protocol section above. If a layer test invokes acute/chronic or context-dependence as a resolution, the rules constrain whether that invocation is valid.
