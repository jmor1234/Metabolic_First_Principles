# Ray Peat — Reading Guide

**What this is:** A prioritized reading order for Ray Peat's articles at raypeat.com, designed for deep study with agent-assisted real-time stress testing against the metabolic framework vault. Articles are ranked by how much integrative reasoning they contain — the ones where he connects the most variables in a single argument come first, because that's where his thinking is most visible and most valuable.

**How to use it:** Read each article carefully, then work through it with an agent holding the vault's full context. Go paragraph by paragraph where it matters. For each key claim: does the vault agree? If not, why not? If the vault doesn't address it, should it? Let the friction between his reasoning and the vault's corrected version teach both the biology and the integration simultaneously.

**What to produce per article:** A concise document capturing: what he's seeing, what's confirmed, what's corrected, what's new, and what reasoning chains are most instructive. Over time this becomes your deep understanding of the integration.

## Notes on Reading Strategy

**Start with Tier 1 articles 1-3.** These give you the thesis, the disease attractor, and the master throttle. If you internalize these three, you'll have the conceptual foundation for everything else.

**The most important article for understanding what was corrected** is #8 (Protective CO₂ and aging). Read it specifically to understand WHY Peat thought CO₂ was the causal driver — the reasoning is compelling, which is exactly why it was wrong in a way that took a formal causal independence audit to catch.

**The most vindicated article** is #6 (Serotonin). His serotonin position was his most contrarian claim and is now his most confirmed by mainstream evidence (Moncrieff 2022).

**The richest article for seeing his integration skill** is probably #2 (When energy fails) or #3 (Thyroid, insomnia, and the insanities) — these are where he moves between topics most fluidly.
