# Project Overview

## What This Project Is

This project is an attempt to understand what health and disease actually are at the most fundamental level biology currently allows — not as a list of conditions and treatments, but as a single system operating from first principles.

It produced three Obsidian vaults. The first was the investigation. The second is the knowledge that investigation produced. The third is the refinements that emerged from stress testing the second.

---

## The Core Question

The question that started this project was not "how do I treat X condition?" or "what supplement should I take?" It was more fundamental: **what IS health?** Not what contributes to it, not what correlates with it — what is it, at the deepest resolution? And the inverse: **what IS disease?** Not which diseases exist, but what disease itself is as a biological phenomenon.

The working answer, arrived at through the investigation documented here, is that health is efficient oxidative metabolism of glucose to CO2, and disease is its progressive failure. Every chronic condition — cancer, diabetes, heart failure, neurodegeneration, autoimmune disease, metabolic syndrome — shares the same metabolic signature: cells shifting from oxidation to glycolysis, mitochondrial function declining, energy production falling. This convergence is not coincidental. It is definitional.

Whether this answer holds up to future evidence is an open question. The project is designed to be corrected, not defended.

---

## Why Peat Was the Starting Point

No single framework was expected to be completely correct. But an investigation of this scope needed a starting point — a coherent integration to test against evidence, rather than assembling fragments from scratch across dozens of disconnected specialties.

Ray Peat provided that starting point. Not because he was right about everything (he wasn't), but because his body of work represented the most coherent first-principles integration of metabolic biochemistry available — one that operated at the deepest mechanistic level (mitochondrial respiration, not symptoms) and covered the broadest range of interconnected topics. Critically, his work was freely available online in its original form, making systematic extraction and verification possible.

Peat was a vehicle for understanding what's most true, not the destination. The investigation was designed to verify, refine, and where necessary correct his positions — not to confirm them. The resulting knowledge stands or falls on the evidence, not on any person's authority.

## The Peat Claims Verification Vault

**Location:** `peat_claims_verified/`

**What it is:** A systematic, claim-by-claim verification of Ray Peat's core biochemistry against modern peer-reviewed research. 16 topic documents covering PUFA, thyroid, estrogen, progesterone, serotonin, CO2, lactate, histamine, endotoxin, liver, sugar, iron, vitamin E, aspirin, calcium, and circadian biology. Plus structural documents (System Overview, Practical Convergence, methodology).

**How it was built:** Each topic went through a three-phase process:

1. **Phase 1 — Extract the actual position.** Read Peat's primary sources and reconstruct his actual reasoning chains with full context. Not summaries or second-hand accounts — the original arguments in their original logic. This matters because most criticism of Peat (and most advocacy) works from caricatures rather than his actual positions.

2. **Phase 2 — Understand the research landscape.** Before verifying, understand the context surrounding the research: who funds it, what comparisons are being made, what timescales are measured, what endpoints are chosen, what disciplinary fragmentation exists. This context is as important as the findings themselves, because a study's design determines what it can and cannot show. Every factual claim about the research landscape was sourced from real-time research, not training data.

3. **Phase 3 — Verify against the evidence.** Compare Peat's position against the best available research with full context. Use an honest assessment scale: confirmed, confirmed with nuance, partially supported, overstated, speculative, not supported. Report all outcomes accurately.

**What it found:** The integration held up better than expected — but not uniformly. The core thesis (health is oxidative metabolism, disease is its failure) is confirmed by converging independent evidence. Most specific mechanisms were confirmed in direction but refined in route — Peat got the clinical outcome right but attributed it to the most direct mechanism available, when the actual mechanism was more upstream or metabolite-mediated ("right destination, slightly wrong route"). Several claims were corrected outright: lactate excitotoxicity contradicted (acute lactate is neuroprotective), pregnenolone steal mechanism wrong (HPA axis suppression, not substrate competition), ASPREE showed aspirin increasing cancer mortality in elderly. All corrections are documented.

The most significant finding across the entire investigation was not about any single mechanism. It was that the evidence for an integrated metabolic picture already exists in the peer-reviewed literature — scattered across specialties that don't cite each other. Cardiology, endocrinology, hepatology, oncology, neurology, immunology, and cell biology all have confirmed pieces of the puzzle. No discipline has assembled them. The integration itself is the contribution.

**What it is for:** The Peat vault is the audit trail. It shows the work — what was claimed, what the evidence says, where the two agree and disagree, and why. It's organized around Peat's topics because it was designed to verify his positions. A reader who wants to know "was Peat right about X?" finds their answer here.

## The Evolution

Once the verification was complete, a problem became clear: the verified knowledge was trapped inside the verification structure. To understand what's true about estrogen, you had to read a document organized as "Peat said X → research says Y → assessment Z." The knowledge was there, but it was inseparable from the scaffold used to produce it.

The intent was never to build a monument to one person's framework. It was to find what's most fundamentally true. So the natural next step was to extract the verified truth and present it as what it is: standalone knowledge about how metabolism works, organized around the biology itself, independent of who originally said what.

This is what the Metabolic Foundations vault is. Not a rewrite of the Peat vault — an evolution from it. The scaffold is removed. The knowledge remains.

## The Metabolic Foundations Vault

**Location:** `metabolic_foundations/`

**What it is:** A first-principles understanding of what health and disease are at the metabolic level. 14 documents, ~55,000 words, 333+ cross-references, 342+ sourced citations. Zero references to Ray Peat or any single person's framework. Includes a verification methodology governing how the vault is tested, evaluated, and changed.

**How it's organized:** Four tiers.

**Tier 1 — Structural.** Two documents that provide ~80% of the conceptual understanding:
- *Foundation* — What health and disease are. The thesis, the first-principles argument, the independent evidence, the honest challenges, and why the integration doesn't exist in mainstream medicine.
- *The System* — How the system produces two self-reinforcing stable states. The two attractors, 15 components, 13 feedback loops in 5 architectural layers, emergent properties (why disease is progressive, why single interventions fail, why context determines effect), and the 24-hour oscillation between states.

**Tier 2 — Mechanisms.** Nine documents covering the detailed biology, grouped by how the biology actually works — not by legacy topic structure:
- The Master Throttle (thyroid)
- The Preferred Substrate (glucose, the Randle cycle)
- The Metabolic Fork (CO2 and lactate as active outputs)
- Membrane Damage and Defense (PUFA, iron, and vitamin E as one peroxidation chain)
- The Hormonal Axis (estrogen and progesterone as a single metabolic switch)
- Conservation Signaling (serotonin and histamine as parallel stress mediators)
- The Gut-Liver Axis (endotoxin and the liver as one anatomical pathway)
- The Cellular Readout (calcium distribution as direct metabolic indicator)
- The Temporal Dimension (the circadian oscillation between attractors)

**Tier 3 — Application.** One document translating the biology into action:
- *Practice* — Eight interventions ordered by leverage, aspirin as detailed pharmacological profile, unified monitoring framework, and implementation sequence distinguishing what matters most from what you can start today.

**Tier 4 — Meta.** Two documents making the vault self-documenting:
- *Methodology* — Evidence standards, confidence calibration, navigation strategy, document template, and the process for expansion.
- *Verification Methodology* — How the vault is tested, verified, and changed: the default position, three hypotheses for evaluating apparent contradictions, the bar for changes, the six-step verification sequence, three rules for handling acute/chronic and context-dependence, and research standards. Distilled from stress-testing the vault's architecture and protecting its integration from degradation during revision.

**How it differs from the Peat vault:**
- No claim-by-claim structure — organized by mechanism
- No Peat quotes, no assessment callouts about his accuracy
- No references to any person as authority
- 16 topics regrouped into 9 mechanism documents following biological reality (PUFA+iron+vitamin E as one chain, estrogen+progesterone as one axis, serotonin+histamine as parallel mediators, endotoxin+liver as one pathway)
- Aspirin moved from mechanism to practice (it's an intervention, not a biological process)
- Evidence cited directly to researchers and journals
- Honest calibration through confidence scales and `[!warning]` callouts, not claim verdicts

**What it is for:** A reader who wants to understand what health and disease actually are — from first principles, backed by evidence, with honest acknowledgment of what's uncertain — starts here. They don't need to know who Peat was, what he claimed, or whether he was right. They just need the knowledge.

## What the Project Found

Across both vaults, the investigation arrived at findings that transcend any single topic:

1. **Health is one thing.** Not many things in balance — one process (oxidative metabolism) whose efficiency determines everything downstream. The hormones, the organs, the immune system, the psychology — all operate through metabolic capacity.

2. **Disease is self-reinforcing.** Thirteen feedback loops interlock to create two stable states. Once established, each state maintains itself through its own outputs. This explains why chronic disease is progressive, why single interventions plateau, and why recovery requires addressing multiple inputs simultaneously.

3. **The evidence already exists.** The research confirming this integration is published across endocrinology, hepatology, oncology, neurology, cell biology, and immunology. No discipline has assembled the pieces. Five structural factors — disciplinary fragmentation, financial architecture, diagnostic proxies, chemical conflation, and premature closure via meta-analysis — explain why.

4. **The framework is honestly limited.** Some mechanisms were corrected. Some claims were contradicted. Context-dependence means the same intervention can help or harm depending on metabolic state. The acute/chronic distinction means the same molecule serves opposite functions at different timescales. These limitations are documented, not hidden.

5. **Time is a dimension of the system.** The organism oscillates between attractors every 24 hours. Long-term trajectory is determined by the balance of daily time spent in each state — how deep the nightly conservation dip goes and how completely the morning reversal restores oxidative metabolism. Health is not a fixed state. It is a daily achievement.

## The Metabolic Refinements Vault

**Location:** `metabolic_refinements/`

**What it is:** The refinements layer. A standalone knowledge layer that captures refinements to the Metabolic Foundations framework discovered through stress testing and other independent triangulation. **Does not modify Metabolic Foundations** — the original integration stays intact, and the refinements sit beside it as a separate epistemic layer. A reader who wants the most current understanding reads both vaults.

**Why it exists separately:** The Verification Methodology in Metabolic Foundations codifies an asymmetry — the cost of wrongly changing an integration that took rigorous work to build is higher than the cost of wrongly leaving it unchanged. Stress testing surfaces real refinements, but those refinements should not silently overwrite the original. They should sit beside it. This vault is that layer.

**How it's organized:** Working files (audit trail) plus standalone refinement documents (knowledge artifacts).

- **Working files** preserve *how* the refinements were derived: `Stress Test.md` (the live investigation log, layer-by-layer adversarial probes against the framework's load-bearing claims) and `Vault Edit Proposal.md` (the diff-form catalog of edits the stress test would apply if the original were overwritten — preserved here as the precise file/passage-level reference).
- **Standalone refinement documents** present *what* each refinement is, in a form a reader can engage with without following the entire investigation chain. Each follows a six-section template: original claim → what was found → refined claim → structural consequences → open questions → audit trail.

**Current contents (refinement documents so far):**
- `Convergence Point Reading.md` — clarifies that the framework's ontological claim is "metabolism as universal convergence point," not "strong reduction." Adds Huntington's as a non-circular example. Source: stress test Sub-test 1.A.
- `Krebs-Cycle Invariance and Ketone Bodies.md` — softens "of glucose" specificity toward "Krebs-cycle CO₂ production as the invariant"; introduces ketone bodies as a biochemically distinct third fuel category; distinguishes Randle pathology from adapted ketosis. Source: stress test Sub-test 1.B (six findings).
- `Loop 7 — Metabolism-Mediator Coupling.md` — replaces direct CO₂→pH→granule mechanism with ATP→V-ATPase→granule pH→V₁-V₀ sensor coupling. Structural consequence: no formalized loop in the 13-loop architecture has CO₂ as causal driver after this refinement. Source: stress test Sub-test 2.A.

**Where the stress test currently is:** Layer 1 complete (REFINEMENT substantial). Layer 2 in progress — Sub-test 2.A complete (REFINEMENT substantial mechanism-level), Sub-test 2.B not yet started. Layers 3, 4, 5 not started. Layer 6 subsumed into the protocol via Rules 1–3 of the Verification Methodology.

**What it is for:** Anyone who wants to know "what does the stress test currently say about claim X" starts here. Anyone who wants the original integration as written starts in Metabolic Foundations. Both are canonical for their own role.

## Where It Goes From Here

The Metabolic Foundations vault is designed to expand. The methodology defines evidence standards, a document template, and a five-step process for adding new mechanism topics. Potential additions include cholesterol, dopamine/prolactin, NAD+ metabolism, altitude, pregnenolone/DHEA, the microbiome beyond endotoxin, and connective tissue.

The Metabolic Refinements vault is designed to grow as the stress test progresses through Layers 2.B, 3, 4, and 5 — and as other independent triangulation produces refinements. Each completed sub-test produces working-file updates plus (where the classification is REFINEMENT or stronger) a new standalone refinement document.

The Peat vault remains as the verification archive — the audit trail showing how the knowledge was produced and where the evidence led.

All three vaults are living documents. The `last-verified` date on every document in Metabolic Foundations signals when its evidence was last checked. The `status` field on Metabolic Refinements documents signals whether each refinement is `proposed` or `accepted` within that vault. As new research emerges, documents are updated. Corrections are features, not failures. The project improves by finding where it's wrong, not by defending where it might be right.

## A Note on How to Read This

Both vaults are integrations. The meaning of any specific claim emerges from its connections to other claims through the wikilink network — not from the words of any single statement read in isolation. Surface terms have deep implications that only become visible when the reasoning chain is traced through the connections. Reading individual statements at face value, without that tracing, produces conclusions the vault does not actually support — and proposed "corrections" that would make the vault less accurate, not more.

The Metabolic Foundations vault includes a `Verification Methodology.md` document codifying the discipline for evaluating evidence and proposing changes without degrading the integration. It exists because the project learned that the cost of wrongly changing the vault is higher than the cost of wrongly leaving it unchanged: a wrong change degrades an integration that took rigorous work to build, while a wrong non-change leaves a possible improvement unmade — but the vault can always be updated later when the evidence is clear. The asymmetry favors caution. The framework is designed to be corrected, but the bar for correction is high.
