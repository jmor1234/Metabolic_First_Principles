# Approach: Reading Peat's Articles Against the Framework

## Purpose

Work through Peat's most important articles one by one to accomplish two parallel goals:

1. **Build deep first-principles understanding and interconnected intuition** for the aspects that matter most — Peat's core reasoning and rationale, internalized correctly.
2. **Keep the vault (`metabolic_framework/`) as accurate from first principles as possible** — use Peat's original reasoning as a stress test on the current integration.

Bi-directional: Peat challenges the vault, the vault challenges Peat. Both get sharper.

## Working Mode

For each article:

1. Read Peat as he actually reasoned. Extract the **first principle** and the **intuition** he was tracking — what system-level truth is he pointing at, and why does the move he's making make sense to him.
2. Parse his claims against the current unified vault.
3. Assign each meaningful claim one of three verdicts:

   - **Confirmed** — reasoning and destination both hold. Absorb directly.
   - **Right destination, wrong route** — direction is right, molecular mechanism is wrong or imprecise. Trust the intuition, carry the corrected mechanism. (Most common case. Most important to keep straight.)
   - **Contradicted on something that matters** — flag hard. Do not let it silently enter the intuition.

4. Only go deep into molecular mechanism when a specific correction changes the picture enough that skipping it would leave the wrong intuition. Otherwise stay at the level of *what is this move, why does it matter, where does it sit in the system*.

## Depth Policy

- **Default**: high-level first principles, core reasoning, interconnections between mechanisms.
- **Drop into molecular detail only when**: a mechanism correction is load-bearing for intuition, or a claim is important enough that getting it wrong would distort downstream reasoning.
- **Do not** get lost in nitty-gritty biochemistry for its own sake. The goal is accurate intuition, not encyclopedic recall.

## What Matters Most

The claims worth the most attention are the ones that:

- Sit on the **structural level** (thesis, attractors, feedback architecture) rather than isolated molecular details.
- **Connect across mechanisms** — where Peat is drawing a line between domains that mainstream medicine keeps separate. That integration is his highest-value contribution.
- Have **downstream leverage** — getting them right (or wrong) propagates into many other conclusions.
- Touch **known corrections** (CO₂-vs-ATP, pregnenolone steal, acute-vs-chronic lactate, unconditional bistability, etc.) — these are where Peat's original framing is most likely to mislead.

## Vault Changes

Vault edits are governed by `metabolic_framework/Verification Methodology.md`:

- **Default position**: the vault is correct until proven otherwise with verified, replicated evidence that directly measures the variables the vault's claims operate on.
- Apparent contradictions between Peat and the vault usually resolve by deeper reading, not by editing.
- Propose a change only when a deep read of both confirms the vault is missing or imprecise on something real. Then trace the three hypotheses and the verification sequence before editing.
