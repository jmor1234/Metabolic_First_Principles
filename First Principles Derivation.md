# First Principles Derivation — Question and Macro Approach

**Status:** Planning document. The derivation itself has not been started. This document captures the foundational question and the high-level approach for running an independent, from-scratch derivation as an experiment — separate from the existing `metabolic_foundations/` and `peat_claims_verified/` vaults.

**Why this exists:** The two existing vaults are the product of verifying Ray Peat's integrative biochemistry against modern research. They contain real, verified knowledge. But their *shape* — the topics covered, the emphases, the vocabulary, the blind spots — was inherited from Peat's source material even after extraction. The verification process was bound by what Peat wrote about; topics Peat didn't emphasize are under-covered or absent, regardless of their actual importance. This experiment asks: if we derive an answer to the foundational question from physical and biological first principles, without consulting Peat or the existing vaults, what do we land on? And how does that compare to what the current vaults say?

The experiment is not a rebuild. It is an independent triangulation. Agreement between the two derivations is strong evidence. Disagreement is a signal that one or both are missing something. Topics in one and not the other are gap evidence for both sides.

---

## The Foundational Question

> **What determines a human organism's functional capacity — its capacity to produce energy, think clearly, move, heal, resist damage, adapt, reproduce, and experience — across a lifetime? Derived from physical and biological first principles, building from molecular processes to lived experience, and treating the level of description at which the answer lives as part of what needs to be derived.**

This is the committed question. Any sub-questions or specific investigations must be derivable from or in service of this question.

---

## Why This Framing

The question was selected through an explicit comparison against four alternative framings. The comparison is preserved in the conversation history; what matters here is what this framing does and doesn't do:

**It decomposes cleanly.** "Functional capacity" is not one thing — it is at least eight distinct capacities that can each be traced and analyzed independently: energy production, cognition, movement, repair, damage resistance, adaptability, reproduction, and experience. The derivation has a clear structure: one thread per capacity, then integration across threads.

**It does not prejudge where the answer lives.** The question asks at what level of description the answer most fundamentally lives — but treats that as something the derivation must produce, not assume. Energy production is molecular/cellular. Cognition is neural/cognitive. Adaptability is systems/behavioral. Experience is phenomenal. The derivation must engage with each level honestly and report where the most fundamental determinants actually sit, rather than committing in advance to metabolism, information, structure, signaling, or any other single level.

**It does not import the medical model.** The question frames capacity positively, not as the absence of disease. This matters because the mainstream medical framing ("health = no diagnosable pathology") distorts what is actually being asked about. Capacity is what you *can* do; disease is one mechanism by which capacity is lost. The derivation starts with capacity and asks what maintains it, rather than starting with disease and asking what prevents it.

**It has a clear termination condition.** The derivation is complete when each of the eight capacities has been traced from first principles to functional expression, the cross-capacity integration has been done, the level-of-description question has been answered honestly, and the bridge to practice has been drawn.

**It connects naturally to practice.** Capacity-building is the action layer by construction. Once the derivation identifies what determines a capacity, "what to do" follows directly — no additional inference layer is required.

**It handles the "is metabolism the right answer" question honestly.** The existing vault commits to efficient oxidative metabolism as the foundational level. A first-principles derivation might land there. It might land somewhere else. It might land on multiple levels with explicit interdependencies. The question is pitched below any of these answers so the derivation can land where the evidence actually leads.

## The Rules of the Derivation

These constraints are what make the derivation independent rather than re-statement of existing work.

1. **Do not consult `metabolic_foundations/` during the derivation.** Not `Foundation.md`, not `The System.md`, not any mechanism document, not `Practice.md`, not `Methodology.md`, not `Verification Methodology.md`. The experiment is defeated if any of these are read before the derivation is complete.

2. **Do not consult `peat_claims_verified/` during the derivation.** Not the system overview, not the topic documents, not the practical convergence document.

3. **Do not use Ray Peat's work as a source or organizing frame.** Not the raypeat.com primary sources, not interviews, not the verification vault's extraction of his claims.

4. **Do not use any synthesis document downstream of Peat.** This includes Peat-derivative work by others and any summary of Peat's positions.

5. **Use primary literature, modern textbooks, and the AI's underlying knowledge of physics / chemistry / cell biology / biochemistry / physiology / systems biology as the source.** Specific claims should cite primary literature. Foundational principles can draw on textbook knowledge.

6. **Document uncertainty honestly.** Where the derivation hits a gap, where it runs into conflicting evidence, where the chain of reasoning is weaker than the surface suggests — say so. The goal is not to produce a clean answer; it is to produce an honest one.

7. **Preserve independence for comparison.** The derivation must remain uncontaminated by the existing vaults until it is complete, so that the later comparison is meaningful. Contamination makes the experiment worthless.

## The Macro Approach

Seven steps at the highest level. Each step is a milestone in the derivation, not a literal section heading. The actual structure of the derivation document may differ, but the logical sequence matters.

### Step 1 — Ground the foundation

Establish what physics and thermodynamics tell us about any living system. What is a living organism, thermodynamically? What does its continued existence actually require? What is the relationship between order, entropy, energy, and time for a self-maintaining system? This is the bedrock that every subsequent layer builds on. It does not require reference to any specific biological framework.

### Step 2 — Specify the capacities to be explained

The question identifies eight capacities: produce energy, think clearly, move, heal, resist damage, adapt, reproduce, experience. Each becomes a first-class target of the derivation. Before tracing them, define each operationally — what does it mean, in measurable or observable terms, for this capacity to be present and functioning? Some capacities may turn out to be special cases of others (e.g., "think clearly" may be a special case of "produce energy in neurons plus maintain signal integrity"). The derivation decides this honestly during Step 4, not in advance.

### Step 3 — Derive each capacity's mechanistic substrate from the bottom up

For each capacity, build the causal chain from physical first principles upward: what does physics require? What does chemistry require? What does cell biology require? What does biochemistry require? What does physiology require? What does systems biology require? What does environmental biology require? The chain terminates when the capacity is fully explained at the level of lived function. This is where most of the derivation's volume lives, and it produces eight parallel traces.

### Step 4 — Integrate across capacities

Once each capacity has been derived independently, look for common substrates, shared mechanisms, and cross-capacity dependencies. Does one capacity depend on another (e.g., does cognition depend on energy production)? Are there shared foundational requirements across all of them? What is the structure of those dependencies — strict hierarchy, partial hierarchy, or genuinely heterarchical? This is where the level-of-description question starts getting answered from evidence rather than speculation.

### Step 5 — Identify where the answer most fundamentally lives

Based on the cross-capacity integration, determine honestly at what level of description the answer most fundamentally lives. Possible outcomes include:

- **Single-level answer.** One level (e.g., energy sufficiency, information integrity, structural coherence, signaling fidelity, homeostatic adaptability) is genuinely foundational, and every capacity's most fundamental determinant sits there.
- **Hierarchical answer.** Multiple levels matter, but they form a clear hierarchy where some levels are more fundamental than others in a specifiable sense.
- **Heterarchical answer.** Multiple levels matter and no single level is most fundamental; different capacities have different foundational levels.
- **Composite answer.** The most fundamental level is itself a compound property (e.g., "sustained energetic availability with intact signaling coherence") that cannot be reduced to a single named level.

Any of these is a legitimate outcome. The derivation must report which one the evidence supports, not which one is aesthetically cleaner.

### Step 6 — Bridge to practice

Translate the derivation into what maintains or builds each capacity. What environmental inputs — food, light, movement, stress, sleep, social connection, temperature, others — affect which capacities through which mechanisms? The practice layer should emerge from the derivation rather than being tacked on. A practice claim that cannot be traced back to the derivation is not a valid output of this experiment.

### Step 7 — Compare to the existing vault (only after the derivation is complete)

Once the independent derivation is finished and documented, compare it to the Metabolic Foundations vault. Three questions to answer:

- **Where do they agree?** Agreement is strong evidence — two independent derivations landing on the same claim is much stronger than either alone.
- **Where do they disagree?** Disagreement is a flag. One or both is missing something. Either can be wrong; the investigation of the disagreement is the next layer of work.
- **What topics does each have that the other doesn't?** Gaps on either side are evidence of either genuine omission or genuine emphasis. The pattern of gaps is informative about what inheritance shaped each derivation.

This comparison is the whole point of the experiment. It is where the information lives. Everything before Step 7 is instrumental to making Step 7 meaningful.

## Termination Conditions

The derivation is complete when all of the following are true:

- Each of the eight capacities has been traced from physical first principles through biological mechanisms to functional expression.
- The cross-capacity integration has been done, with dependencies between capacities made explicit.
- The level-of-description question has been answered honestly and explicitly.
- The bridge to practice has been drawn for each capacity.
- The result is a standalone document that could be read by someone with no exposure to the existing vaults or to Peat and make complete sense on its own terms.
- The comparison against the existing vault has been run and documented.

Until all six conditions are met, the derivation is still in progress.

## Scope and Tractability

The derivation is a significant undertaking but not unbounded. The eight capacities are finite. The layers (physics → chemistry → cell biology → biochemistry → physiology → systems → environment → individual) are finite. The integration step is finite. The comparison is finite. What makes it tractable is that each capacity is a separate thread and the threads can be worked independently before integration.

Estimated scope: the derivation document is likely to be comparable in size to the existing `metabolic_foundations/` vault (tens of thousands of words), but the work required to produce it is less than the work that produced the existing vault, because the verification methodology is already in place and the AI capabilities required for cross-specialty synthesis are now mature. The reference class is weeks-to-months of focused work, not years.

## What This Document Is NOT

- **Not the derivation itself.** The actual bottom-up derivation will happen in a separate document (or set of documents) after this planning doc is approved.
- **Not a commitment to scrap the existing vaults.** The existing vaults contain real verified work. Nothing is being deleted, and nothing will be deleted regardless of what the derivation produces. The derivation is a parallel, independent artifact.
- **Not a rejection of Peat's contribution.** Peat did the integration work at human-alone speed in a pre-AI era, and much of what he integrated is holding up under verification. His contribution was historically significant. The point of this experiment is not to replace him; it is to see what an independent derivation produces when the AI-enabled capability envelope is used for the synthesis work directly.
- **Not a stress test of the existing vault.** The stress test (see `Stress Test.md`) is a different methodology — it probes the existing vault's load-bearing claims against modern evidence. The first-principles derivation is upstream of that: it asks what the vault would look like if it had been built without inheriting its organizing frame from any single person.

## Relationship to the Stress Test and the Vault Edit Proposal

The stress test of the existing vault (Layers 1, 2.A, and pending 2.B, 3, 4, 5) and the `Vault Edit Proposal.md` that captures its committed changes are a **separate and parallel** workstream from this first-principles derivation. Both are legitimate. They serve different purposes:

- The **stress test + edit proposal** takes the existing vault as given and probes its internal integrity, correcting specific load-bearing claims against modern evidence. It strengthens what is already there.
- The **first-principles derivation** ignores the existing vault and derives an answer to the foundational question independently, so that the two derivations can be cross-checked. It reveals what the existing vault's shape inherited from its sources vs. what it derived from the biology.

Both workstreams can run in parallel. Neither replaces the other. The eventual goal is to use the comparison (Step 7 of the derivation) plus the stress test results to produce a vault that is both internally verified and externally cross-checked against an independent derivation.
